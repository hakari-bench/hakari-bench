## Overview

`ja` is the Japanese split of NanoMLDR. Japanese questions retrieve long
Japanese articles containing the answer-bearing paragraph.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[M3-Embedding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03216) evaluates MLDR as a
multilingual long-document retrieval benchmark and reports that handling long
inputs is a core part of the task. The [MLDR dataset card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/Shitao/MLDR)
lists Japanese as Wikipedia-sourced and describes GPT-3.5 question generation
from sampled paragraphs.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 148 queries, 3,112 documents, and 148 positive qrels. Each
query has one positive. Queries average 51.70 characters and documents average
5,384.62 characters, shorter than most European-language splits in character
count. Examples include climbing, tanks, criminal justice, television programs,
and military organization.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.7590 and hit@10 = 0.8446. It ranks 101 positives first. Japanese tokenization
and terminology can affect lexical retrieval, but many generated questions keep
distinctive article terms.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes Japanese Wikipedia question-article pairs,
Japanese long-document QA retrieval, multilingual MLDR training data, and hard
negatives sharing titles, era terms, or technical vocabulary.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic data should generate Japanese questions from a paragraph in a long
article and use the full article as the positive. Hard negatives should share
the title domain or named entities but not answer the question.
