## Overview

`es` is the Spanish split of NanoMLDR. It evaluates retrieval of long Spanish
documents for paragraph-grounded Spanish questions.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[M3-Embedding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03216) describes MLDR as a benchmark
for multilingual long-document retrieval using articles from Wikipedia, Wudao,
and mC4. The [MLDR dataset card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/Shitao/MLDR)
lists Spanish sources as Wikipedia and mC4, with questions generated from
sampled article paragraphs.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 176 queries, 3,312 documents, and 176 positive qrels. Each
query has one positive. Queries average 120.26 characters, and documents average
12,539.90 characters. The examples are long Spanish questions over articles
about marine steam engines, Marvel characters, cinema, municipalities, and early
modern treaties.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.8998 and hit@10 = 0.9489, with 150 positives ranked first. This is one of the
easiest observed NanoMLDR splits for BM25 because the generated questions often
reuse distinctive Spanish terms from the target article.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes Spanish Wikipedia question-article pairs,
Spanish mC4 long-document retrieval, multilingual MLDR training data, and hard
negatives from same-topic articles with overlapping names and dates.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic data should generate specific Spanish questions from paragraphs within
long articles. Negatives should share the main entity or domain but not the
answer-bearing paragraph.
