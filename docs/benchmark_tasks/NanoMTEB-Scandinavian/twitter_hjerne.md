## Overview

`twitter_hjerne` is a Danish social-media answer retrieval task. Queries are
Danish #twitterhjerne question tweets, and documents are human reply tweets.
The retriever must find relevant answers to the question tweet.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The source dataset card for `sorenmulli/da-hashtag-twitterhjerne` describes
Danish questions asked on Twitter/X using the hashtag `#Twitterhjerne`
("Twitter brain") and their answers. It states that each question tweet has
2-6 answer tweets. Søren Vejlgaard Holm's thesis describes the dataset as a
small set of tweets asking for help or input, manually filtered to have a clear
question, no required attached photo, no personal information, and at least two
relevant replies.

SEB adapts this into retrieval by using the question tweet as the query and the
human replies as relevant answer documents.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 77 Danish queries, 262 documents, and 262 positive qrels.
Queries average 165.01 characters, and documents average 128.49 characters.
Almost every query is multi-positive: 75 of 77 queries have more than one
positive, with a median of 3 relevant answers.

The data is informal social Danish. Topics include technology help,
recommendations, travel, food, children's activities, shopping, and household
problems. Tweets may contain hashtags, URLs, abbreviations, emojis, and spelling
variation.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.2395 and hit@10 = 0.6104. BM25 ranks a positive first for 24 queries and
retrieves at least one positive in the top 10 for 47 queries. The task is hard
for lexical retrieval because useful answers often do not repeat the question's
wording.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes Danish social QA, forum question-reply pairs,
customer/community support threads, and multi-positive retrieval examples.
Training should keep multiple human replies as positives rather than collapsing
to a single canonical answer.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate informal Danish help-seeking tweets and multiple plausible replies.
Include recommendations, short factual help, tool tips, and subjective advice.
Hard negatives should be plausible replies to nearby topics but not useful for
the specific question.
