## Overview

`tv2_nordretrieval` is a Danish local-news retrieval task derived from
Nordjylland News / TV2 Nord summarization data. Queries are short news
summaries, and documents are the corresponding full news articles.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The Danish Foundation Models datasheet describes Nordjylland News as articles
from the Danish newspaper TV2 Nord, derived from the Hugging Face dataset
`alexandrainst/nordjylland-news-summarization` and originally intended for text
summarization. Søren Vejlgaard Holm's thesis also uses Nordjylland News as a
Danish summarization scenario and describes it as TV2 Nord newspaper texts with
corresponding summaries.

SEB converts news summarization data into retrieval by using summaries or
headlines as queries and matching articles as corpus documents.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 Danish queries, 2,048 documents, and 200 positive qrels.
Every query has one positive. Queries average 127.97 characters, and documents
average 1,440.57 characters. Sampled items cover local politics, accidents,
sports clubs, culture, and nature.

Queries read like short article summaries, not keyword titles. Documents are
full local-news articles with quotes, background details, and event chronology.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.8957 and hit@10 = 0.9350. BM25 ranks 171 positives first. The summary
queries often reuse central names, places, and event terms from the article, so
lexical retrieval is strong.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Danish news summary/article pairs,
headline-to-article retrieval, and local-news hard negatives from the same
municipality or sports team. Training should exclude this Nano split and any
TV2 Nord article/summary duplicates.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Danish local-news articles with concise summaries. Include named
municipalities, teams, officials, dates, and quotes. Hard negatives should share
the same location or topic but describe a different event.
