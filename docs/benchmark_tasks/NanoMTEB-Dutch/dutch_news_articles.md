## Overview

`dutch_news_articles` is a Dutch news retrieval task from MTEB-NL. Queries are
NOS-style news article titles, and documents are Dutch news article bodies or
summaries. The task asks a model to retrieve the matching news article for a
headline-like query, testing Dutch lexical coverage, named-entity handling, and
event-level matching in a current-affairs domain.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[MTEB-NL and E5-NL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12340) includes
DutchNewsArticlesRetrieval as one of the native Dutch retrieval tasks used to
broaden Dutch embedding evaluation beyond translated BEIR datasets. The dataset
metadata points to the public Dutch News Articles Kaggle record, described as
articles scraped from the NOS website, one of the major Dutch news
organizations, as of January 1, 2010.

No standalone retrieval paper was confirmed for this task. The interpretation
here relies on the MTEB-NL paper, the Kaggle source record, the Hugging Face
source dataset card, repository metadata, and observed Nano rows.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows.
Every query has exactly one positive. Queries are short Dutch headlines with an
average length of 48.96 characters. Documents average 1,146.66 characters and
look like NOS article text covering politics, public safety, health, transport,
international news, and social issues.

The sampled positives show mostly clean title-to-article matching: for example,
a headline about a Paris radio-building fire retrieves an article about the
Maison de la Radio fire, and a headline about electric scooters retrieves an
article with sales statistics from RAI Vereniging and Bovag. Some articles are
longer than ordinary FAQ answers and include several entities, so a model must
retain the central event rather than match only a single named entity.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8868
and hit@10 = 0.9350. This is a high lexical baseline. News headlines often share
distinctive entities and nouns with the article body, and the single-positive
setup rewards exact event identification.

The remaining difficulty is mostly in paraphrase and context: short headlines
may omit the full article terminology, while documents can contain several
people, places, and secondary events. Dense retrieval should improve mainly
where the headline expresses the event in fewer or different words than the
article.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Dutch headline-to-article pairs,
Dutch news search logs, title-body pairs from public news archives, and
multilingual news retrieval data carefully adapted to Dutch. Any source likely
to contain the same NOS articles, titles, or positive body text should be
excluded or audited before training for this benchmark.

Because this is a single-positive task, training should include hard negatives
from the same news topic or same named entity so that models learn event-level
matching instead of retrieving any article about the same person or institution.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, start from non-evaluation Dutch news articles
and generate concise Dutch headlines that preserve the main event, actors, and
location without copying long article phrases. Include same-topic hard negatives
with overlapping entities.

For joint generation, create short Dutch headlines and realistic article bodies
about politics, sport, safety, health, culture, and international news. Each
headline should map to one clear article, and generated negatives should be
nearby but not answer the same news event.
