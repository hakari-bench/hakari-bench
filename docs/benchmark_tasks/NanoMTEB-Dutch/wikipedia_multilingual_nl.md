## Overview

`wikipedia_multilingual_nl` is the Dutch subset of WikipediaRetrievalMultilingual
from MTEB-NL. Queries are Dutch synthetic information-seeking questions
grounded in Wikipedia articles, and documents are Dutch Wikipedia-style
passages. The task tests broad encyclopedic retrieval in Dutch.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[MTEB-NL and E5-NL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12340) describes
WikipediaRetrievalMultilingual as a multilingual retrieval dataset constructed
from queries generated by a multilingual LLM grounded in Wikipedia articles and
designed to resemble SQuAD-style retrieval. The source metadata points to
`ellamind/wikipedia-2023-11-retrieval-multilingual-queries`, which includes a
Dutch `nl` test configuration, and to MTEB's `WikipediaRetrievalMultilingual`.

No standalone paper was confirmed for this exact dataset. The interpretation
below is based on the MTEB-NL appendix, the Hugging Face source metadata, and
observed Nano samples.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows.
Every query has exactly one positive. Queries average 63.54 characters and are
Dutch questions about Wikipedia facts. Documents average 381.01 characters and
are concise Wikipedia-style passages.

The examples ask about Dutch army mortars, Mu'tazilites, Belgian federal debt,
a 1985 Vienna agreement, and Lineas locomotives. The questions are clean and
answer-seeking, but their synthetic origin means they may be more directly
grounded in the positive passage than organic search questions.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8444
and hit@10 = 0.9250. This is a high lexical baseline: synthetic questions often
contain entities or terms that appear in the positive passage.

The remaining difficulty comes from relation matching and passage selection
among similarly themed Wikipedia text. A model must retrieve the exact passage
that answers the question, not just a page about the same entity.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Dutch Wikipedia question-passage
pairs, multilingual Wikipedia retrieval datasets, SQuAD-style synthetic QA pairs
created from non-evaluation passages, and Dutch encyclopedic search data. Avoid
the MTEB test queries, positives, and qrels used in this Nano split.

Because every query has one positive in the Nano sample, single-positive
training with strong entity-near hard negatives is appropriate.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation Dutch Wikipedia passages
and generate factual questions answerable from explicit statements in the
passage. Vary question types across which, why, when, who, and what.

For joint generation, create concise Dutch encyclopedia passages with named
entities, dates, quantities, and relations, then generate questions grounded in
those facts. Hard negatives should mention the same entity or topic but not
answer the requested relation.
