## Overview

`vabb` is a Dutch and Flemish academic-bibliography retrieval task from MTEB-NL.
Queries are publication titles, and documents are bibliographic records or
abstract-like descriptions from the Flemish Academic Bibliography for the Social
Sciences and Humanities. The task tests retrieval in humanities and social
science publication metadata.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The [VABB-SHW Zenodo record](https://zenodo.org/records/14214806) describes the
fourteenth edition of the Flemish Academic Bibliography for the Social Sciences
and Humanities as a database of approved publications by researchers affiliated
with Flemish universities. It notes that the database is used as one parameter
in the Flemish performance-based research funding system.

[MTEB-NL and E5-NL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12340) uses this source to build
a retrieval task by matching a subsample of titles with corresponding abstracts
or bibliographic descriptions. No standalone retrieval paper for VABBRetrieval
was confirmed.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 9,123 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows.
Every query has exactly one positive. Queries average 74.47 characters and are
academic titles, often in Dutch but sometimes containing English, French, Latin,
or named entities. Documents average 837.89 characters and may include author,
year, publication metadata, abstract text, or topical description.

The examples cover criminology, archaeology, jihadism, spatial planning, and
psychopathy. Some positive documents are short bibliographic records rather than
full abstracts, while others contain long descriptive text.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.6952
and hit@10 = 0.7850. Lexical overlap between title and bibliographic description
helps, but many humanities titles are metaphorical or concise, and documents may
describe the same work with author metadata and broader disciplinary wording.

The single-positive setup makes near-miss bibliographic records costly. Models
must distinguish the exact publication from other records in the same topic or
discipline.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping title-to-abstract pairs from
Dutch, Flemish, or multilingual academic bibliographies, citation metadata,
library search logs, and humanities/social-science paper metadata. The VABB
records, titles, and qrels used in the Nano split should be excluded from
training.

Because the task is single-positive, hard negatives from the same author,
discipline, publication year, or title keywords are especially useful.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation academic abstracts or
bibliographic records and generate plausible publication titles in Dutch,
English, or multilingual Flemish academic style. Preserve disciplinary terms and
named entities.

For joint generation, create bibliographic records with title, authors, year,
discipline, and abstract-like description, then use the title as the query.
Include hard negatives from similar disciplines or author groups.
