## Overview

`web_faq_nld` is the Dutch subset of WebFAQRetrieval from MTEB-NL. Queries are
natural FAQ-style questions or short web search prompts, and documents are
answer snippets from FAQ pages. The task tests broad-coverage Dutch
question-answer retrieval over real web FAQ language.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[WebFAQ: A Multilingual Collection of Natural Q&A Datasets for Dense Retrieval](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.20936)
describes WebFAQ as 96 million natural question-answer pairs across 75
languages, derived from FAQ-style schema.org annotations in Common Crawl. The
paper reports filtering for near duplicates and semantic consistency and frames
the collection as multilingual dense-retrieval data with broad domain coverage.

[MTEB-NL and E5-NL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12340) uses the Dutch subset of
WebFAQRetrieval as provided by MTEB. This task is therefore web-derived FAQ
retrieval, not a curated single-domain QA benchmark.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows.
Every query has one positive. Queries average 50.45 characters and documents
average 322.18 characters. The sample includes information-security
certification, clothing advice, car rental duration, caravan stickers, and pond
UV lamps.

The data is broad and visibly web-like. Some examples include commercial or
template-like phrasing, and one positive answer contains English text for a
Dutch query. This makes the task useful for practical web FAQ retrieval but also
noisier than a domain-controlled benchmark.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.7698
and hit@10 = 0.8450. FAQ questions often share key terms with their answers,
which helps lexical retrieval.

The remaining difficulty comes from short, underspecified questions and noisy
web phrasing. Answers may restate the question only indirectly, include
commercial boilerplate, or mix languages, so semantic matching and robustness to
web text are useful.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Dutch FAQ pairs, multilingual
WebFAQ training data permitted by benchmark rules, customer-support QA pairs,
and website FAQ retrieval data. Training should exclude the Dutch test rows,
answers, and qrels used in this Nano split.

Since every query has a single positive in this Nano sample, training should
focus on answerability and hard negatives from the same website or product
domain.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation Dutch FAQ answers and
generate natural user questions that the answer resolves. Preserve the variety
of web domains: services, shopping, travel, civic procedures, technical help,
and consumer advice.

For joint generation, create realistic FAQ entries with question and answer
pairs, plus same-site hard negatives. Include occasional concise answers,
policy-like answers, and commercial phrasing, but keep the answer explicitly
grounded.
