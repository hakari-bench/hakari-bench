## Overview

`belebele_eng_latn_nld_latn` is the English-to-Dutch Belebele retrieval split:
Dutch questions retrieve English passages. It evaluates cross-lingual semantic
alignment from Dutch reading-comprehension queries to English answer-bearing
documents.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[The Belebele Benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.16884) introduces a parallel
multiple-choice reading-comprehension dataset covering 122 language variants.
The paper states that each passage comes from FLORES-200 and that the benchmark
contains 900 unique questions with four answer choices. Its parallel design
allows monolingual and cross-lingual evaluation across the same underlying
questions and passages.

[MTEB-NL and E5-NL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12340) includes BelebeleRetrieval
as a Dutch retrieval task and says that the Dutch portion was used as provided
without additional preprocessing. In the retrieval adaptation, the question is
used as the query and the corresponding passage is the relevant document.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 488 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows. Each
query has one positive. Queries average 69.39 characters and are Dutch
questions, while documents average 475.51 characters and are English passages.
The sampled rows ask about constitutional amendments, a president's medical
condition, a gas leak, an Afghan runoff election, and a radio show.

The task is short-query, short-passage retrieval, but it is not simply bilingual
dictionary matching. The query often asks for a relation or exclusion, such as
which reason was not used, so models need both cross-lingual alignment and
reading-comprehension-style semantic matching.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.4738
and hit@10 = 0.5850. This is much stronger than many cross-lingual retrieval
settings because named entities, numbers, and some cognates survive across Dutch
and English, and the candidate pool is small at 488 documents.

Still, BM25 only finds a positive in the top 10 for 117 of 200 queries. It can
benefit from words such as "Arias", "Macbeth Street", or "2015", but it has no
direct lexical route for ordinary Dutch phrases like "gaslek" or "hertelling"
when the evidence is in English.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes Dutch-to-English parallel QA retrieval, translated
reading-comprehension examples, multilingual NLI or QA pairs converted to
retrieval, and sentence-aligned Dutch-English corpora. Training should exclude
the Belebele test questions and passages used by this split.

Because each query has one positive, contrastive training with English hard
negatives from nearby passages is appropriate. Models should learn to bridge
Dutch question phrasing to English factual passages rather than memorizing
Belebele templates.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation English passages and
generate Dutch questions that are answerable from the passage. Include
questions that ask for locations, causes, exclusions, chronology, and
description, matching the observed Belebele style.

For joint generation, create short English news or encyclopedic passages and
Dutch comprehension questions. Add hard negatives that share entities or topic
words but do not answer the question.
