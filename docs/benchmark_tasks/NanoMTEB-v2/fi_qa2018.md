## Overview

`fi_qa2018` is a financial question-answer retrieval task. Queries are finance
questions, and relevant documents are answers or answer-like passages from a
financial QA collection.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FiQA](https://doi.org/10.1145/3184558.3192301) was created for financial
opinion mining and question answering. The retrieval task tests whether a model
can match investor and personal-finance questions to useful answer passages.
[MTEB](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07316) includes FiQA2018 as an English
retrieval benchmark.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 534 positive qrels. Queries
average 61.70 characters. Documents average 780.39 characters and include
financial advice, explanations, and personal-finance reasoning. Positives
average 2.67 per query, with a median of 2 and up to 12 positives for one query.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.3385 and hit@10 = 0.5950. It ranks 69 queries with a positive first, and the
median best positive rank is 4. The task is moderately difficult for lexical
retrieval because answers may use finance-specific terms while the question is
short, informal, or scenario-based.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes financial QA pairs, investor-forum question-answer pairs,
personal-finance FAQ retrieval data, and hard negatives from the same financial
topic or product type.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate realistic personal-finance questions and answer passages that mention
taxes, brokerage accounts, ETFs, bank transfers, credit, or employer stock.
Hard negatives should share the same product or market term but answer a
different decision.
