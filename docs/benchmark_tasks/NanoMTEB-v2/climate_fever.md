## Overview

`climate_fever` is a Climate-FEVER hard-negative retrieval split. Queries are
real-world climate claims, and documents are Wikipedia evidence passages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[CLIMATE-FEVER](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.00614) adapts the FEVER evidence
retrieval and verification setup to real-world climate claims collected from the
web. It asks systems to retrieve evidence that supports, refutes, or fails to
support climate-change claims. [MTEB](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07316) includes
Climate-FEVER as a retrieval dataset; this NanoMTEB-v2 split uses the MTEB
hard-negative source dataset `mteb/ClimateFEVER_test_top_250_only_w_correct-v2`.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 621 positive qrels. It is
strongly multi-positive: queries average 3.10 positives, 181 of 200 queries
have more than one positive, and the maximum is 5. Queries average 114.97
characters. Documents average 1,115.93 characters and are Wikipedia passages,
with some long pages and occasional non-English fragments in the candidate pool.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.1934 and hit@10 = 0.4750. Only 95 of 200 queries have any positive in the top
10, and the median best positive rank is 12. The hardest cases are claims that
use climate talking-point language while the evidence appears under broader
Wikipedia topics such as global warming, weather, marine mammals, or agriculture.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping climate claim-evidence pairs,
FEVER-style evidence retrieval, science fact-checking data, and hard negatives
with similar climate terms but different evidential targets.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate climate claims with quantities, dates, and attribution language, then
pair them with Wikipedia-style evidence passages. Include positives that support
and refute claims, and hard negatives that share words like `temperature`,
`emissions`, or `ice` without being evidence for the claim.
