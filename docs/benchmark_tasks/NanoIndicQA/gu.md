## Overview

`NanoIndicQA / gu` is the Gujarati split of IndicQA retrieval. Gujarati
questions retrieve Gujarati evidence paragraphs from a small context corpus.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The source paper [Towards Leaving No Indic Language Behind](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.05409)
introduces IndicXTREME and the IndicQA reading-comprehension benchmark. MTEB
repurposes the task for retrieval by pairing each question with its context.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 248 documents, and 201 positive qrel rows. One
query has two positives. Queries average 61.01 characters and documents average
960.50 characters. The examples show history and city-description passages.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.6060
and hit@10 = 0.6900. It ranks 105 positives at rank 1 and 138 in the top 10.
All positives are within the top 100.

### Training Data That May Help

Gujarati context QA, Gujarati Wikipedia retrieval, and multilingual Indic QA
pairs will help. Hard negatives should be paragraphs about nearby people,
places, or historical periods.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Gujarati questions from context paragraphs and include several
questions per paragraph. Use city, history, biography, number, and date facts,
and retain full paragraphs as positives.
