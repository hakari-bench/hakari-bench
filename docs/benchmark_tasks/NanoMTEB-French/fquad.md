## Overview

`fquad` is the French FQuAD retrieval split. Queries are French reading
comprehension questions, and documents are French Wikipedia passages. The
retriever must find the passage containing the answer evidence.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FQuAD: French Question Answering Dataset](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.06071)
introduces a native French reading-comprehension dataset built from French
Wikipedia, with more than 25,000 samples in FQuAD1.0 and more than 60,000 in
FQuAD1.1. The paper follows a SQuAD-style setup but uses French-native
questions and answers.

The source dataset for this Nano split is `manu/fquad2_test`.
[FQuAD2.0](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.13209) extends FQuAD with adversarial
unanswerable questions, but this retrieval task uses answer-bearing passages as
positives.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 French queries, 269 documents, and 200 positive qrels.
Every query has one positive. Queries average 56.21 characters, and documents
average 898.31 characters. Sampled questions ask about spirituality, Pierre
Lambert de La Motte, Breton socialism, and Sadi Carnot.

Documents have article-title prefixes and compact encyclopedic paragraphs,
which makes them much shorter and cleaner than Alloprof or BSARD.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.8990 and hit@10 = 0.9550. BM25 ranks 165 positives first. The task is
lexically favorable because questions often reuse distinctive article terms or
answer-bearing phrasing.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping FQuAD train examples, French
Wikipedia QA retrieval pairs, and same-article hard negatives. Training should
exclude FQuAD test examples, Nano queries, qrels, and positive passages likely
to overlap.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate French Wikipedia-style paragraphs and extractive questions whose
answer is explicitly stated in the paragraph. Include biographies, history,
science, and mythology. Hard negatives should come from the same article or
related entities.
