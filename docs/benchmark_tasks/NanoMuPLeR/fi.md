## Overview

`NanoMuPLeR / fi` is the Finnish split of MuPLeR-retrieval. It uses synthetic
Finnish legal questions and Finnish DGT-Acquis-derived EU legal passages. The
retrieval target is the one passage that contains the legal condition or factual
detail required by the query.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The [MuPLeR-retrieval dataset card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mteb/MuPLeR-retrieval)
defines the benchmark as multilingual parallel legal retrieval with 10,000
human-translated EU legal passages and 200 synthetic queries per language. [An overview of the European Union's highly multilingual parallel corpora](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10579-014-9277-0)
is the cited source reference for DGT-Acquis and related EU corpora.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 single-positive qrels.
Queries average 160.16 characters, the longest mean among the sampled MuPLeR
languages, while documents average 683.64 characters. Examples concern capital
duty rates, state-aid compensation criteria, procurement evaluation, Euratom
policy, and pre-accession reduction clauses.

### BM25 Difficulty

BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.6706 and hit@10 = 0.7400, with 119 positives at rank 1
and 148 in the top 10. Finnish morphology and longer query wording make lexical
matching less dominant than in several other splits, though numbers and EU
institution names remain useful anchors.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping Finnish EU legal retrieval pairs, Finnish
legal QA, EUR-Lex passages, and multilingual parallel legal data. Avoid the
MuPLeR evaluation query-passage pairs and near-duplicate aligned passages.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Finnish questions from non-evaluation EU legal passages, keeping dates,
percentages, treaty concepts, and institutions explicit. Include hard negatives
from nearby legal provisions that share vocabulary but answer a different
condition.
