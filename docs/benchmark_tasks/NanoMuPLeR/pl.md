## Overview

`NanoMuPLeR / pl` is the Polish split of MuPLeR-retrieval. It uses synthetic
Polish legal questions and Polish EU-law passages derived from DGT-Acquis. The
task is single-positive retrieval of the passage that grounds the legal answer.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The [MuPLeR-retrieval dataset card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mteb/MuPLeR-retrieval)
documents a multilingual legal retrieval dataset based on DGT-Acquis passages
and synthetic aligned queries. [An overview of the European Union's highly multilingual parallel corpora](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10579-014-9277-0)
is the source-corpus reference for the EU multilingual parallel resources.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 200 Polish queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 positive qrels.
Queries average 143.97 characters and documents average 686.12 characters.
Sampled queries ask about capital duty, state-aid compensation, supplier
capacity in procurement, Euratom priorities, and pre-accession reduction rules.

### BM25 Difficulty

BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8400 and hit@10 = 0.9050, with 154 positives at rank 1
and 181 in the top 10. The Polish split has strong lexical overlap through
legal terms, dates, and percentages, but a model still needs to match the full
condition in the query.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping Polish EU legal retrieval, Polish EUR-Lex
passages, Polish legal QA, and multilingual legal bitext. Avoid MuPLeR
evaluation query-passage pairs and exact translated equivalents.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Polish legal questions from non-evaluation EU passages, preserving
precise dates, thresholds, institutional actors, and legal consequences.
Construct hard negatives from related legal passages that share terms but do not
answer the same question.
