## Overview

`NanoMuPLeR / pt` is the Portuguese split of MuPLeR-retrieval. It evaluates
retrieval of Portuguese DGT-Acquis-derived EU legal passages from synthetic
Portuguese legal questions. Each query has exactly one positive passage.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The [MuPLeR-retrieval dataset card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mteb/MuPLeR-retrieval)
describes a multilingual parallel legal retrieval benchmark based on DGT-Acquis
passages and synthetic parallel queries. [An overview of the European Union's highly multilingual parallel corpora](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10579-014-9277-0)
is the cited source reference for the EU parallel-corpus family.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 200 Portuguese queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 positive qrels.
Queries average 135.46 characters and documents average 702.90 characters.
Sampled queries cover import taxes, state-aid compensation, procurement
capacity, nuclear-policy priorities, and pre-accession reduction clauses.

### BM25 Difficulty

BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8222 and hit@10 = 0.8950, with 146 positives at rank 1
and 179 in the top 10. The task is lexically friendly because questions and
passages share formal legal terms, numeric rates, and dates, but matching the
full legal condition still matters.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping Portuguese EU legal retrieval pairs,
Portuguese EUR-Lex text, multilingual legal QA, and hard negatives from related
EU provisions. Exclude MuPLeR evaluation positives and aligned equivalents.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Portuguese legal questions from non-evaluation EU passages. Preserve
percentages, dates, treaty concepts, and institutional roles, and add hard
negatives with overlapping topic vocabulary but different legal answers.
