## Overview

`NanoDAPFAMOutTitlAbsToTitlAbs` is a compact cross-domain patent-family
retrieval task. Both query and target use title plus abstract text, and
positive citation targets do not share IPC3 classes with the query.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[DAPFAM: A Domain-Aware Family-level Dataset to benchmark cross domain patent retrieval](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22141)
highlights OUT-domain retrieval as a difficult setting where relevant prior art
comes from another technical class. This split is the shortest representation of
that setting, with only title and abstract on both sides.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 1,257 positive qrels.
Queries average 786.64 characters and documents average 777.87 characters.
Positives per query average 6.29.

The compact representation makes the benchmark close to semantic patent-summary
retrieval. Since IPC3 classes differ, the positive relation often depends on
technical analogy rather than direct field vocabulary.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column and best-ranked positive per
query, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.1079 and hit@10 = 0.1750. BM25 ranks 12
positives first and finds a positive in the top 100 for every query.

The low hit@10 shows that lexical title-abstract matching is brittle when
relevance crosses domain boundaries.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes cross-domain patent abstracts linked by citations,
technology analogy search, and prior-art retrieval pairs where target IPC/CPC
classes differ from the source.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate short patent summaries in different domains and pair them by shared
mechanism or material rather than identical terminology. Include same-domain
lexical distractors as hard negatives.
