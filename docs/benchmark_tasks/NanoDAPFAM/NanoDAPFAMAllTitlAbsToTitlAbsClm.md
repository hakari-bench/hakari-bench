## Overview

`NanoDAPFAMAllTitlAbsToTitlAbsClm` retrieves title-abstract-claims patent-family
records from title-abstract queries. It uses all DAPFAM citation positives,
including both in-domain and out-of-domain patent-family relations.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[DAPFAM: A Domain-Aware Family-level Dataset to benchmark cross domain patent retrieval](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22141)
studies patent prior-art retrieval with multiple query and corpus
representations. This split asks whether a compact patent summary can retrieve a
target record that includes claims, which add legal scope and detailed technical
constraints to the candidate text.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 3,989 positive qrels.
Every query has multiple positives, averaging 19.95 positives. Queries average
776.02 characters, and target documents average 7,230.62 characters.

The query side is concise, while the target side contains enough claim language
to include both useful technical anchors and repeated legal boilerplate. This
puts pressure on models to align a short invention summary with longer claim
scope.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column and the best-ranked positive
per query, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.6648 and hit@10 = 0.8450. BM25 ranks 98
positives first and places at least one positive in the top 100 for every query.

The BM25 score is high for an all-domain patent task, likely because title and
abstract terms align well with target claims while claims provide additional
lexical evidence.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes title-abstract queries against claim-enriched
patent documents, prior-art search, and citation-linked patent-family pairs.
Avoid using the NanoDAPFAM evaluation family IDs, qrels, or duplicate family
members during training.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate compact source patent summaries and target patent records with claims.
Include positives that are cited prior art or technical continuations, plus hard
negatives with similar claims but different invention scope.
