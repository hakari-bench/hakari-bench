## Overview

`NanoMTEB-Thai / miracl_th` is the Thai MIRACL hard-negative retrieval split.
Thai questions retrieve Thai Wikipedia passages from a 10,000-document candidate
pool. The task evaluates Thai passage retrieval with many hard negatives.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Making a MIRACL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.09984) describes MIRACL as a
multilingual monolingual retrieval benchmark over Wikipedia passages, with
native-speaker relevance judgments. Thai is one of the languages inherited from
the Mr. TyDi/TyDi lineage. The MTEB hard-negative version pools top documents
from BM25 and neural systems, producing a difficult candidate set.

This split measures same-language Thai factual search, but with a harder
negative distribution than a random corpus sample.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 343 positive qrel rows.
There are 86 multi-positive queries. Queries average 43.59 characters and
documents average 471.83 characters. The examples ask about Thai royal history,
music, Confucius, Thai law, and artists.

The documents are Thai Wikipedia-like passages, usually starting with an entity
title. Several queries have multiple acceptable passages, so the task rewards
finding any judged evidence passage but still requires good ranking among
near-topic negatives.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.5737
and hit@10 = 0.8150. It ranks 86 positives at rank 1 and finds a positive in the
top 10 for 163 of 200 queries. All positives are within the top 100.

BM25 is strong because many Thai queries repeat names and dates. Failures often
come from entity-neighbor confusion, such as retrieving another British prime
minister page for a question about Winston Churchill in 1951.

### Training Data That May Help

Thai MIRACL training data, Thai Wikipedia QA retrieval, and hard negatives from
the same article or entity family are useful. Training should exclude overlapping
MIRACL dev/test items and should preserve Thai tokenization and date variants.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Thai fact questions from non-evaluation Wikipedia passages. Include
queries about dates, offices, biographies, definitions, and named works. Add hard
negatives from related entities or same-topic passages so models learn
passage-level evidence selection.
