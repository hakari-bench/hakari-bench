## Overview

`NanoMTEB-Thai / web_faq_tha` is a Thai WebFAQ retrieval split. Natural Thai FAQ
questions retrieve their corresponding answer passages gathered from web FAQ
pages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[WebFAQ](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.20936) describes a large multilingual
collection of natural question-answer pairs mined from FAQ pages on the web. The
MTEB card describes WebFAQRetrieval as broad-coverage FAQ retrieval across many
languages. In this split, both questions and answers are Thai.

The task measures practical FAQ matching over web text, including commercial,
service, product, and support questions.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows.
Each query has one positive answer. Queries average 43.88 characters and
documents average 224.32 characters. The examples include online casino FAQs,
delivery/location questions, membership questions, and product descriptions.

The corpus is broad and web-like. Some questions include mixed Latin/Thai brand
names, and answers may be promotional or service-oriented rather than neutral
encyclopedia text.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.6501
and hit@10 = 0.7450. It ranks 112 positives at rank 1 and 149 in the top 10. All
positives are in the top 100.

BM25 works well when question and answer repeat brand names or FAQ terms. The
harder cases are paraphrased policy questions where many promotional answers
share similar words.

### Training Data That May Help

Thai FAQ retrieval, multilingual WebFAQ training data, customer-support QA, and
Thai web service question-answer pairs are useful. Training should preserve
brand names, mixed scripts, and short direct answers.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Thai FAQ questions and answers for services, products, accounts,
payments, delivery, promotions, and support. Include mixed Thai/Latin brand
names and terse answers. Use hard negatives from other FAQ answers in the same
site or service category.
