## Overview

`touche2020_vn` is the Vietnamese Touché 2020 argument-retrieval task from
VN-MTEB. Queries are translated controversial questions, and documents are
translated argumentative passages from debate-style sources. The task tests
whether a retriever can find relevant pro or con arguments, not just documents
that mention the topic.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Overview of Touché 2020: Argument Retrieval](https://ceur-ws.org/Vol-2696/paper_261.pdf)
describes the first CLEF Touché shared task on argument retrieval. Task 1 asks
systems to retrieve relevant arguments from a focused crawl of debate portals
for controversial social topics. The overview notes that argument retrieval
requires both topical relevance and argument quality, and that simple
argumentation-agnostic baselines remained competitive.

[BEIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08663) includes Touché 2020 as an argument
retrieval task. [VN-MTEB](https://aclanthology.org/2026.findings-eacl.86/)
translates and filters the source data into Vietnamese, preserving the
controversial-question retrieval setup.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 25 queries, 10,000 documents, and 481 positive qrel rows.
Every query has multiple positives; the average is 19.24 positives per query,
with a range from 6 to 32. Queries average 52.20 characters. Documents average
1,939.62 characters and have a very long tail up to 64,898 characters.

Observed examples ask whether obesity is a disease, whether the federal minimum
wage should increase, whether sexual orientation is determined at birth, whether
universal basic income should be provided, and whether capital punishment should
be allowed. Positive documents are long debate turns or argument passages.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.7369
and hit@10 = 1.0000. Since every topic has many positives and strong topical
keywords, BM25 always retrieves at least one positive in the top 10.

The challenge is ranking high-quality and on-point arguments above long,
topic-adjacent debate text. A high hit rate does not mean the top result covers
the most useful stance or reasoning.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Touché argument-retrieval data,
argument mining corpora, Vietnamese debate and opinion QA, stance-labeled
arguments, and translated argument retrieval data with overlap removed. The
translated test topics, qrels, and positive argument passages used by this Nano
split should be excluded.

Because all queries are multi-positive, training should use multi-positive or
listwise ranking and should model stance diversity where possible.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation debate passages and
generate Vietnamese controversial questions that the argument addresses.
Preserve stance, claim, premises, and policy details.

For joint generation, create controversial questions with multiple pro and con
arguments, plus same-topic but off-stance or low-quality hard negatives. Do not
seed generation from this evaluation split.
