## Overview

`nfcorpus_vn` is the Vietnamese NFCorpus retrieval task from VN-MTEB. Queries
are translated lay health and nutrition information needs, and documents are
translated biomedical article abstracts. The task tests medical information
retrieval across a large lexical gap between everyday health wording and
scientific PubMed-style evidence.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[A Full-Text Learning to Rank Dataset for Medical Information Retrieval](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-30671-1_58)
builds NFCorpus from NutritionFacts.org pages and research articles, mainly from
PubMed. The paper states that relevance links are extracted at three levels from
direct and indirect links between lay health content and scientific articles,
specifically to bridge lay-language queries and medical literature.

[BEIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08663) uses NFCorpus as a biomedical retrieval
task. [VN-MTEB](https://aclanthology.org/2026.findings-eacl.86/) translates and
filters the source data into Vietnamese; observed queries still include some
short or non-Vietnamese remnants, while documents are overwhelmingly
Vietnamese-translated abstracts.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 166 queries, 3,618 documents, and 4,571 positive qrel rows.
It is strongly multi-positive: the average is 27.54 positives per query, the
median is 14, and 93.98% of queries have multiple positives. Queries average
24.72 characters and can be very short, such as a food or disease name.
Documents average 1,584.25 characters and are long biomedical abstracts.

Observed examples include Crohn's disease diet treatment, galactosemia, milk,
pork, and healthy vegetables. The positive documents can be scientifically
indirect, reflecting the original weak/graded relevance links from lay health
topics to biomedical articles.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.3105
and hit@10 = 0.6084. The hit rate is moderate, but nDCG is low for a task with
many positives per query. Short lay queries such as "sữa" or "Thịt lợn" are
lexically sparse, while abstracts use detailed biomedical terminology.

The median first relevant BM25 rank is 3, so lexical retrieval often finds a
positive early but struggles to order the many relevant medical abstracts.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes official NFCorpus training data where permitted,
Vietnamese consumer-health QA, medical abstract retrieval pairs, PubMed-style
query-document relevance data, and translated biomedical retrieval data with
overlap removed. The translated test queries, qrels, and positive abstracts used
by this Nano split should be excluded.

Because most queries have many positives, multi-positive or listwise training is
more faithful than one-positive contrastive training.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation biomedical abstracts and
generate Vietnamese lay health or nutrition queries that a non-expert might ask.
Preserve medical terms, interventions, outcomes, populations, and measurements.

For joint generation, create realistic biomedical abstracts and lay queries with
graded relevance, plus hard negatives from the same disease, nutrient, or
intervention family. Do not use evaluation split passages as seeds.
