## Overview

`fever_vn` is the Vietnamese FEVER retrieval task from VN-MTEB. Queries are
translated factual claims, and documents are translated Wikipedia evidence
passages. The task tests whether a retriever can find evidence pages or
passages that support or refute a claim before any final fact-verification
decision is made.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FEVER: a large-scale dataset for Fact Extraction and VERification](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05355)
introduces 185,445 claims generated by altering Wikipedia sentences and then
verifying them against Wikipedia. The paper reports supported, refuted, and not
enough information labels, and requires evidence sentences for supported or
refuted claims.

[BEIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08663) casts FEVER as a fact-checking
retrieval task: claims are queries and evidence passages are documents.
[VN-MTEB](https://aclanthology.org/2026.findings-eacl.86/) translates and
filters the source data into Vietnamese, producing this Vietnamese evidence
retrieval split.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 232 positive qrel rows.
Most queries are single-positive, but 29 queries have multiple positives and the
maximum is 3. Queries average 56.03 characters. Documents average 392.40
characters and are compact Wikipedia-style entity passages.

Observed claims concern historical succession, film casting, screenwriting,
gender or personhood, and music release dates. The positives usually contain
the named entity page and enough context for verifying the claim.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8805
and hit@10 = 0.9500. Exact entity names such as Henry III of France, Amy Adams,
Warren Beatty, or Little Mix are strong lexical anchors, and the median first
relevant rank is 1.

This high lexical baseline means the task mainly stresses entity-preserving
translation and evidence ranking. More difficult cases arise when the claim is
true or false because of a relation that is not copied verbatim in the evidence.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes official FEVER training claims and evidence where
permitted, Vietnamese claim-evidence retrieval data, Wikipedia entity evidence
pairs, and translated FEVER-style data with overlap removed. The translated test
claims, qrels, and positive evidence passages used by this Nano split should be
excluded.

Training should distinguish retrieval from final verification: memorizing claim
labels does not teach evidence retrieval.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation Vietnamese Wikipedia
passages and generate factual claims that are supported or refuted by explicit
evidence in the passage. Preserve dates, names, occupations, releases, and
relations.

For joint generation, create short entity passages and claims with support,
refutation, and hard-neighbor evidence. Hard negatives should mention the same
entity but fail to prove the target relation.
