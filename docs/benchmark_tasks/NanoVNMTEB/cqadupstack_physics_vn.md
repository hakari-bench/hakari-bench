## Overview

`cqadupstack_physics_vn` is the Vietnamese Physics subforum split of
CQADupStack from VN-MTEB. Queries are translated Physics StackExchange question
titles, and documents are translated archived physics question threads. The task
tests duplicate retrieval for conceptual and mathematical physics questions.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[CQADupStack](https://doi.org/10.1145/2838931.2838934) provides duplicate
question retrieval data from twelve StackExchange communities, including
Physics. It was designed to support reproducible cQA retrieval experiments and
notes that duplicate and non-duplicate pairs have overlapping lexical-similarity
distributions.

[VN-MTEB](https://aclanthology.org/2026.findings-eacl.86/) converts this source
task into Vietnamese using LLM translation plus semantic and judge-based
filtering. The resulting split keeps physics terminology while translating the
question prose.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 592 positive qrel rows.
The average is 2.96 positives per query; 82 queries have multiple positives and
the maximum is 72. Queries average 58.56 characters. Documents average 800.96
characters and often include conceptual explanations, equations in prose, or
thought experiments.

Examples include gravity in a tunnel through Earth, propagation speed of force,
cloud thickness, pushing versus pulling, and negative temperature. These are
conceptual physics duplicates where the same idea may be expressed with very
different surface wording.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.4138
and hit@10 = 0.6100. Lexical retrieval benefits from terms such as gravity,
temperature, cloud, or force, but duplicate physics questions often require
matching the underlying physical setup.

The task rewards semantic abstraction: the relevant duplicate can use a
different scenario to ask the same principle, such as gravity at Earth's center
or absolute negative temperature.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Physics StackExchange duplicate
pairs, Vietnamese physics QA, translated CQADupStack training splits with
overlap removed, and conceptual science paraphrase data. The translated Physics
test questions, qrels, documents, and duplicate clusters used by this Nano split
should be excluded.

Hard negatives should share physics terms but differ in the actual principle or
setup.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation physics QA threads and
generate Vietnamese duplicate titles that ask the same concept in shorter form.
Preserve formulas, quantities, and named physical concepts when present.

For joint generation, create clusters of Vietnamese physics questions around one
principle, with equivalent variants and nearby non-equivalent questions. Include
hard negatives from the same subfield, such as mechanics, thermodynamics, or
electromagnetism.
