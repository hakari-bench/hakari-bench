## Overview

`nano_fever` is the Vietnamese NanoFEVER retrieval task from VN-MTEB. Queries
are translated factual claims, and documents are translated Wikipedia evidence
passages. The task tests evidence retrieval for fact verification: a model must
find passages that can support or refute a claim before any label prediction is
attempted.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FEVER: a large-scale dataset for Fact Extraction and VERification](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05355)
introduces 185,445 claims generated by altering Wikipedia sentences and then
verified against Wikipedia. The paper describes a pipeline in which systems
retrieve evidence, select evidence sentences, and classify claims as supported,
refuted, or not enough information.

[BEIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08663) casts FEVER as a fact-checking
retrieval task with claims as queries and evidence passages as documents.
[VN-MTEB](https://aclanthology.org/2026.findings-eacl.86/) translates and
filters the source data into Vietnamese. This split is the GreenNode
`nano-fever-vn` source variant rather than the separate `fever_vn` split.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 232 positive qrel rows.
Most queries are single-positive, but 29 queries have multiple positives and the
maximum is 3. Queries average 56.03 characters. Documents average 462.25
characters and are Wikipedia-style passages, often beginning with the entity
title.

Observed examples include claims about Georges Gilles de la Tourette, Kevin
Bacon, Deepika Padukone, Netscape Navigator, and medical-school programs. The
positive passages usually contain an entity profile with dates, roles, or
descriptive facts needed to verify the claim.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8574
and hit@10 = 0.9350. Entity names and titles make lexical retrieval strong, and
the median first relevant rank is 1.

The remaining difficulty is relation-sensitive evidence ranking. A passage that
mentions the entity is not always enough; the retriever must rank the page that
contains the specific fact needed for verification.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes official FEVER training claims and evidence where
permitted, Vietnamese claim-evidence retrieval pairs, Wikipedia entity evidence
pairs, and translated FEVER-style data with overlap removed. The translated
NanoFEVER test claims, qrels, documents, and positive passages used by this Nano
split should be excluded.

Training should focus on retrieving evidence passages, not memorizing the final
claim label.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, use non-evaluation Vietnamese Wikipedia
passages and generate factual claims whose support or refutation depends on
explicit dates, names, roles, works, or locations in the passage.

For joint generation, create short entity passages plus supported and refuted
claims, with hard negatives that mention the same entity but omit or contradict
the target relation. Do not use this evaluation split as a synthetic seed.
