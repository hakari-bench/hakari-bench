## Overview

`NanoJMTEB-v2 / nlpjournal_abs_intro` is a Japanese academic retrieval task where
an abstract must retrieve the corresponding introduction section from the same
paper. It is derived from the NLP Journal LaTeX Corpus through JMTEB.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The JMTEB card says the NLP Journal V2 tasks are created by shuffling titles,
abstracts, introductions, and article texts from the Japanese NLP Journal LaTeX
Corpus. In this task, the query side is the paper abstract and the corpus side is
the paper introduction.

This measures fine-grained academic component matching. The query and positive
document discuss the same paper, but they are different sections with different
rhetorical roles: the abstract summarizes contributions and results, while the
introduction motivates the problem and cites background work.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 637 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows. Each
query has one positive introduction. Abstracts average 494.52 characters, and
introductions average 2,148.04 characters.

The examples retain LaTeX markers, citations, section labels, and technical NLP
vocabulary. Compared with abstract-to-full-article retrieval, the documents are
much shorter but still long enough to include background paragraphs and related
terminology shared by many papers.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.6417
and hit@10 = 0.7400. It ranks 107 positives at rank 1 and 148 in the top 10.
All positives appear within the top 100.

BM25 remains strong because abstracts and introductions share technical terms.
It is weaker than abstract-to-full-article retrieval because the introduction
does not always repeat the abstract's experimental details or results.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training includes Japanese academic-paper section matching,
abstract-introduction pairs, and document contrastive learning over papers in
the same NLP subfield. Hard negatives from the same topic area are important
because vocabulary overlap is high across papers.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Japanese NLP-like abstracts and introductions for the same synthetic
paper. Keep the rhetorical difference: abstracts should state the contribution
and result, while introductions should motivate the problem and cite background.
Include hard negatives with similar terminology but different methods.
