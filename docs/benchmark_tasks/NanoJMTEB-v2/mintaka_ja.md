## Overview

`NanoJMTEB-v2 / mintaka_ja` is the Japanese retrieval split derived from
Mintaka. Japanese complex QA questions must retrieve the short answer entity
label from a compact corpus. The task is much closer to entity-answer retrieval
than passage retrieval.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Mintaka: A Complex, Natural, and Multilingual Dataset for End-to-End Question
Answering](https://aclanthology.org/2022.coling-1.138/) introduces Mintaka as a
complex multilingual QA dataset with 20,000 English question-answer pairs
elicited from crowd workers, annotated with Wikidata entities, and translated
into eight additional languages including Japanese. The paper emphasizes complex
question types such as count, comparative, superlative, ordinal, multi-hop,
intersection, difference, and yes/no questions.

The JMTEB card notes that the retrieval task uses the Japanese split and keeps
entity-type answers while avoiding answers that are only numbers or booleans,
following the MTEB setting. In this Nano task, the documents are short answer
labels rather than evidence passages.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 1,592 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows.
Every query has one positive answer label. Queries average 35.19 characters.
Documents average only 9.17 characters, reflecting entity names such as people,
films, songs, and places.

The examples are complex QA prompts translated into Japanese. Some ask for a
comparison, first/last item, lowest budget, or entity meeting a condition. The
retrieval problem is to select the exact entity label, not to retrieve supporting
text. This makes the corpus compact but semantically demanding.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.1312
and hit@10 = 0.1600. It ranks 21 positives at rank 1 and 32 in the top 10. All
positives appear within the top 100.

BM25 is weak because answer labels often do not occur in the query. For example,
a query comparing The Weeknd and Drake must retrieve "ドレイク", and a query
asking for the first MCU film must retrieve "アイアンマン". Lexical overlap is
often absent, so the task needs stored world knowledge or training that maps
complex questions to answer entities.

### Training Data That May Help

Helpful training includes Japanese entity QA, multilingual Mintaka-style QA,
Wikidata-linked question-answer pairs, and entity retrieval supervision where
the answer label is not repeated in the question. Training should avoid the
Mintaka test questions represented in this Nano split.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate complex Japanese questions over non-evaluation entities, then pair each
question with a short answer label. Include comparative, superlative, ordinal,
multi-hop, intersection, and first/last questions. Because the real corpus is
answer labels rather than evidence passages, synthetic negatives should include
nearby entity labels that are plausible but wrong.
