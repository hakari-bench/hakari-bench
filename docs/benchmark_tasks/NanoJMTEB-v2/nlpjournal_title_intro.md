## Overview

`NanoJMTEB-v2 / nlpjournal_title_intro` is a Japanese academic retrieval task
where a paper title must retrieve the corresponding introduction section. It is
derived from the NLP Journal LaTeX Corpus through JMTEB.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The JMTEB card describes the NLP Journal V2 tasks as retrieval tasks over
shuffled paper titles, abstracts, introductions, and full articles. This split
uses the shortest query form, the title, and asks the model to retrieve the
matching introduction.

This is a title-to-section matching task. It measures whether a retriever can
connect a compact technical title to the broader motivation and background
section of the same paper.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 637 documents, and 200 positive qrel rows. Each
query has one positive introduction. Titles average 27.02 characters, and
introductions average 2,148.04 characters.

The observed title queries are short and sometimes highly technical. Documents
contain longer background exposition, LaTeX citation commands, labels, and
domain-specific terminology. This creates a strong mismatch between the small
amount of query text and the longer document context.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.0991
and hit@10 = 0.1100. It ranks 17 positives at rank 1 and 22 in the top 10. All
positives appear within the top 100.

BM25 is weak because the title often shares only a few terms with the
introduction, and many introductions in the corpus discuss related NLP
background. Dense models should improve by mapping title semantics to the paper's
problem setting and method context.

### Training Data That May Help

Japanese and multilingual academic title-to-section retrieval data will help,
especially with hard negatives from the same research area. Training on
title-abstract pairs alone may help less than title-introduction pairs because
the positive document has a different rhetorical structure.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate compact Japanese paper titles and longer introductions. Make
introductions motivate the problem and cite prior work instead of restating the
title directly. Add hard negatives from introductions with overlapping terms and
similar subfields.
