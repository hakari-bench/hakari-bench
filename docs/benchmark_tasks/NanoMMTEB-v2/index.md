## Overview

NanoMMTEB-v2 is a compact retrieval group drawn from multilingual MTEB/MMTEB
retrieval tasks. It is intentionally heterogeneous: legal statute retrieval,
counterargument retrieval, multilingual reading comprehension, Chinese COVID
policy search, FAQ-style answer retrieval, legal bill retrieval, long-context
passkey retrieval, MIRACL, MLQA, scientific related-paper retrieval, spatial
reasoning, StackOverflow QA, StatCan dialogue-to-table retrieval, temporal
reasoning, TREC-COVID, Danish Twitter advice retrieval, multilingual Wikipedia
QA, and WinoGrande-style referent retrieval all appear in one group.

The group is useful as a broad mixed-domain multilingual retrieval probe. It
does not isolate one source benchmark or one language. Instead, it tests whether
models can stay robust across short factual questions, long legal situations,
dialogue logs, scientific titles, code-support questions, synthetic long
contexts, and reasoning-style answer retrieval.

## Details

### What the Original Group Measures

[MMTEB: Massive Multilingual Text Embedding Benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.13595)
expands the MTEB framework to hundreds of multilingual tasks and languages.
[MTEB: Massive Text Embedding Benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07316)
provides the common task interfaces used by the retrieval tasks. NanoMMTEB-v2 is
a Nano-style retrieval subset hosted by hakari-bench that samples a diverse
selection of those retrieval-style tasks.

This group should be read as a mixed benchmark bundle, not a single coherent
dataset family. Some splits are conventional passage retrieval tasks; others
convert reasoning, legal classification, dialogue, or long-context tasks into
retrieval. The common structure is the Nano retrieval interface: queries,
candidate documents, qrels, and dataset-provided BM25 rankings.

### Subtask Coverage

The eighteen subtasks cover seven broad retrieval families:

- **Legal and government retrieval:** `ailastatutes`,
  `legal_bench_corporate_lobbying`, and `statcan_dialogue_dataset` cover legal
  statute matching, corporate lobbying bill retrieval, and dialogue-to-StatCan
  table retrieval.
- **General QA and multilingual evidence:** `miracl`, `mlqa`,
  `wikipedia_multilingual`, `belebele`, `hagrid`, and `covid` cover multilingual
  Wikipedia-style retrieval, reading comprehension, FAQ/answer retrieval, and
  Chinese COVID policy/news retrieval.
- **Scientific and biomedical retrieval:** `scidocs` and `treccovid` cover
  related scientific documents and COVID-19 biomedical literature.
- **Argument and social/code support:** `argu_ana`, `stack_overflow_qa`, and
  `twitter_hjerne` cover counterargument retrieval, developer QA, and Danish
  social advice retrieval.
- **Reasoning retrieval:** `spart_qa`, `temp_reason_l1`, and `wino_grande`
  convert spatial, temporal, and masked-referent reasoning into retrieval.
- **Long-context key lookup:** `lembpasskey` retrieves long documents containing
  passkeys embedded in repetitive context.
- **Mixed-language stress:** `belebele`, `miracl`, `mlqa`,
  `statcan_dialogue_dataset`, and `wikipedia_multilingual` include many
  languages or language variants.

The qrel structure varies by task. Ten splits are single-positive in the
observed Nano data, while eight contain multi-positive queries. TREC-COVID has
the largest per-query qrel counts, with many queries capped at 100 positives.
The global median positives per query is still 1 because most queries are
single-positive.

### Observed Group Profile

Across the eighteen splits, NanoMMTEB-v2 contains 3,248 queries, 9,408 positive
qrels, and 116,569 split-local candidate documents. The document count is a sum
across subtasks, not a deduplicated group-wide corpus size. Queries average
348.67 characters when weighted by query count, and documents average 1,262.66
characters when weighted by split-local document count.

The group has strong length contrasts. `lembpasskey` documents average 28,060.87
characters, `statcan_dialogue_dataset` documents average 7,237.69 characters,
and legal/statute queries can be thousands of characters. At the other end,
`temp_reason_l1` answers average only 9 characters and `wino_grande` answers
average 7.68 characters. The sampled records include long legal facts, baseball
arguments, multilingual Belebele answers, Chinese COVID policy snippets, FAQ
answers, bill descriptions, passkey paragraphs, Arabic MIRACL passages, MLQA
evidence, scientific titles, spatial answer phrases, Laravel answers, StatCan
table metadata, date strings, biomedical abstracts, Danish Twitter replies,
Bulgarian Wikipedia passages, and one-word WinoGrande referents.

### BM25 Difficulty

Query-weighted BM25 nDCG@10 is 0.4089 and query-weighted hit@10 is 0.5545. The
task spread is very large. `lembpasskey` is easiest (nDCG@10 = 0.9963,
hit@10 = 1.0000), followed by `hagrid`, `wikipedia_multilingual`,
`legal_bench_corporate_lobbying`, `treccovid`, and `stack_overflow_qa`.
`statcan_dialogue_dataset` is hardest (nDCG@10 = 0.0111, hit@10 = 0.0300), with
`temp_reason_l1`, `mlqa`, `belebele`, `covid`, and `scidocs` also much harder.

BM25 is strong when the answer document repeats distinctive query terms or when
the corpus is small and direct: passkeys, FAQ answers, legal bill summaries,
Wikipedia multilingual answer passages, StackOverflow answers, and TREC-COVID
biomedical abstracts. It is weak when the target is short and must be inferred
(`temp_reason_l1`), when long dialogue must map to a structured table
(`statcan_dialogue_dataset`), when multilingual reading-comprehension answers
do not share enough surface text (`mlqa` and `belebele`), or when Chinese COVID
queries need policy-specific evidence rather than broad topic overlap.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data must be task-family aware. Legal tasks benefit from statute
retrieval, legal issue-to-law mapping, and bill summary matching. QA tasks need
multilingual evidence retrieval and FAQ answer selection. Scientific and
biomedical tasks need citation/abstract retrieval and COVID literature search.
StackOverflow and Twitter tasks need community-answer retrieval. Reasoning tasks
need spatial, temporal, and coreference-style retrieval data. Long-context tasks
need key-value lookup and answer localization inside long documents.

Training should exclude NanoMMTEB-v2 evaluation queries, qrels, positives, and
source rows used in the Nano splits. Because this group mixes public benchmark
test splits, upstream task data should be audited before use. For multi-positive
tasks such as TREC-COVID, MIRACL, SCIDOCS, StatCan, Twitter Hjerne, and
AILAStatutes, preserve the qrel multiplicity rather than reducing each query to
one arbitrary positive.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic data should mirror each retrieval relation rather than simply mixing
all tasks. Generate legal situations with relevant statutes, dialogue logs with
matching statistical table metadata, multilingual questions grounded in one
passage, biomedical topics with many relevant abstracts, StackOverflow questions
with practical answer posts, and reasoning prompts whose answer is a short
entity, date, or referent.

Hard negatives should share topic and surface vocabulary but fail the exact
relation. For StatCan, a hard negative can share subject and geography but
describe the wrong table dimensions. For MLQA or Belebele, it can be the right
article but the wrong passage. For temporal reasoning, it can be an adjacent
date. For StackOverflow, it can mention the same framework but solve a different
bug.
