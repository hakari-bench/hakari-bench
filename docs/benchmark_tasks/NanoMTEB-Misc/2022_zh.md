## Overview

`NanoMTEB-Misc / 2022_zh` is the Chinese document split of TREC 2022 NeuCLIR.
Chinese topic statements retrieve Chinese news articles from a hard-negative
NeuCLIR candidate set.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Overview of the TREC 2022 NeuCLIR Track](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.12367)
states that NeuCLIR studied ad hoc retrieval over Chinese, Persian, and Russian
Common Crawl news collections. The original task asked systems to bridge from
English topics to target-language news; monolingual translated topics were also
provided as a reference task.

This split uses Chinese queries and Chinese documents. It preserves the broad
TREC-style information needs and relevance pools, so the retrieval target is any
judged relevant news article, not a single paragraph containing a fixed answer.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 47 queries, 10,000 documents, and 1,643 positive qrel rows.
Queries average 24.00 characters and documents average 1,107.60 characters.
Almost every query is multi-positive: 46 of 47 queries have more than one
positive, and the average is 34.96 positives per query.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.2212 and hit@10 = 0.2553. It ranks 11 positives at rank 1, but only 12
queries have a positive in the top 10.

This is the hardest NeuCLIR split in this Nano set for BM25. Short Chinese
queries and broad news topics leave little lexical signal, and the observed
misses show that a top-ranked article can be unrelated to the query despite
sharing high-level news vocabulary.

### Training Data That May Help

Chinese news retrieval, Chinese TREC topic retrieval, multilingual CLIR data,
and hard negatives from same-topic news clusters should help. Tokenization-aware
sparse training can help lexical baselines, but dense semantic alignment is
important for query-document paraphrase.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Chinese search topics from clusters of related news articles, with
several relevant documents per topic. Include hard negatives that share entities
or event categories but differ in the requested relation, impact, or location.
Avoid synthetic examples where every positive repeats the query exactly.
