## Overview

`NanoMTEB-Misc / fi` is the Finnish split of EuroPIRQ retrieval. Finnish
synthetic questions retrieve Finnish DGT-Acquis-derived legal and administrative
passages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The [EuroPIRQ dataset card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/eherra/EuroPIRQ-retrieval)
states that the dataset was constructed from DGT-Acquis paragraph-level text by
extracting, cleaning, language-validating, and selecting aligned English,
Finnish, and Portuguese chunks. It then generated 100 synthetic questions per
language, mapped to corresponding passages.

[MMTEB: Massive Multilingual Text Embedding Benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.13595)
provides the broader multilingual embedding benchmark context. No standalone
EuroPIRQ paper was confirmed; this page uses the dataset card and observed
samples as the task source.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 100 queries, 9,422 documents, and 100 positive qrel rows. Each
query has one positive. Queries average 146.53 characters and documents average
594.55 characters. The sample shows Finnish EU institutional text with formal
legal and committee vocabulary.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.8493 and hit@10 = 0.9200. It ranks 78 positives at rank 1 and 92 in the top
10.

BM25 is strong because many questions retain exact legal entities, directives,
and institutional terms. It is slightly harder than English because Finnish
morphology and legal phrase variation can reduce exact-term overlap.

### Training Data That May Help

Finnish legal retrieval, multilingual EU parallel corpora, and synthetic
question-passage training are useful. Hard negatives should come from similar
EU committee, court, and directive passages.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Finnish questions from non-evaluation EU legal passages. Include both
surface-overlap questions and paraphrased questions that require semantic
matching across inflected Finnish forms. Select hard negatives with similar
directive numbers, institutions, and legal boilerplate.
