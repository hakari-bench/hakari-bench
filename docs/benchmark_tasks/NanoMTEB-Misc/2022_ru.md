## Overview

`NanoMTEB-Misc / 2022_ru` is the Russian document split of TREC 2022 NeuCLIR.
Russian topic statements retrieve Russian news articles from the NeuCLIR
hard-negative retrieval table.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Overview of the TREC 2022 NeuCLIR Track](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.12367)
defines NeuCLIR as a TREC track for neural cross-language information retrieval
over Chinese, Persian, and Russian news. The original ad hoc task used English
topics to retrieve documents in the target language, while monolingual topics
were human translations vetted by language experts.

This Nano split uses Russian query text against Russian news articles. It
therefore measures Russian news retrieval over the same TREC-style information
needs and pooled relevance judgments, with many topics having multiple relevant
documents.

### Observed Data Profile

The split has 44 queries, 8,722 documents, and 1,664 positive qrel rows. Queries
average 85.57 characters and documents average 2,448.87 characters. The task is
strongly multi-positive: the average is 37.82 positives per query, with 42 of
44 queries having more than one positive.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.3182 and hit@10 = 0.7273. It ranks 14 positives at rank 1 and finds a
positive within the top 10 for 32 queries.

BM25 is often helped by named entities and translated topic wording, but the
large relevant pools and long articles mean that a sparse model can retrieve a
related article without retrieving a judged best article early.

### Training Data That May Help

Training on Russian news search, Russian TREC-style topic retrieval, and
cross-lingual English-Russian retrieval will help. Hard negatives should be
same-topic news articles that share event vocabulary but are not judged
relevant to the specific information need.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Create Russian topical search requests from news clusters and pair each request
with several relevant articles. Generate negatives from same entities, same
country, and same broad news category. Keep queries as user information needs,
not factoid questions with one answer.
