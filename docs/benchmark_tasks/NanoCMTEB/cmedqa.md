## Overview

`cmedqa` is a Chinese medical consultation retrieval task. Queries are patient
questions or short case descriptions, and the retriever must find matching
doctor-style answer passages from the CMedQA-derived `CmedqaRetrieval` source
task in C-MTEB.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[C-Pack: Packed Resources For General Chinese Embeddings](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.07597)
introduces C-MTEB as a Chinese embedding benchmark covering retrieval,
reranking, similarity, classification, pair classification, and clustering.
The paper lists `CmedqaRetrieval` among the retrieval datasets used to evaluate
whether Chinese embedding models can retrieve semantically matching evidence
from task-specific corpora. It also names CMedQA-v2 as one of the labeled
Chinese paired-text resources integrated into C-MTP training data.

The MTEB dataset card for [CmedqaRetrieval](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mteb/CmedqaRetrieval)
describes the source as online medical consultation text based on CMedQAv2. No
standalone CmedqaRetrieval paper was confirmed for this page, so the
interpretation here combines the C-Pack benchmark paper, the dataset card, and
the observed Nano rows.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 324 positive qrels.
Queries average 51.99 characters and read like patient complaints or direct
questions to a physician. Documents average 157.57 characters and are concise
medical advice answers. Positive labels are partly multi-valued: 70 of 200
queries have more than one positive answer, with a maximum of 9 positives.

The sampled data includes thyroid treatment, prenatal ultrasound, mental health
symptoms, hemorrhoids, hepatitis antibodies in pregnancy, and other consumer
health topics. Text is mostly Chinese, with occasional punctuation or formatting
noise from online consultation records.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.0113 and hit@10 = 0.0200. Only 4 queries have a positive in the top 10, and
all sampled examples except none are near rank 99 or 100. This is a difficult
lexical baseline setting because patient wording and clinician answers often
use different phrasing, and short answers may omit terms repeated in the query.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Chinese medical QA pairs,
consultation-question to doctor-answer retrieval pairs, disease-symptom
paraphrases, and hard negatives from the same medical specialty. Any training
set should exclude Nano query-answer pairs and near-duplicate consultation
threads.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic data should pair realistic patient complaints with answer passages
that directly address diagnosis, treatment, triage, or follow-up. Hard negatives
should share symptoms or disease names while recommending a different action or
describing a different patient context.
