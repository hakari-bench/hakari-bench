## Overview

`t2` is a Chinese passage-ranking retrieval task derived from T2Ranking.
Queries are short real-world search queries, and documents are Chinese web
passages that may be long, noisy, and partially formatted with HTML.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[T2Ranking: A large-scale Chinese Benchmark for Passage Ranking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03679)
introduces a Chinese passage ranking benchmark with more than 300,000 queries
and more than 2 million unique passages from real-world search engines. The
paper describes expert annotation with four relevance levels, multiple
annotators, candidate diversification, passage segmentation, deduplication, and
active learning to reduce false negatives.

The same paper emphasizes that fine-grained relevance labels and realistic
Chinese web passages make the task challenging for both sparse and neural
retrievers. [C-Pack](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.07597) includes `T2Retrieval`
in C-MTEB's retrieval category.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 10,000 documents, and 979 positive qrels.
Queries average 10.74 characters. Documents are the longest among the observed
NanoCMTEB splits, averaging 913.50 characters, with some passages containing
HTML fragments, copied pages, medical notes, or product-style text.

Positive labels are strongly multi-valued: 175 of 200 queries have multiple
positives, with an average of 4.89 positives and a maximum of 23. This makes the
task closer to passage ranking over a candidate collection than a single-answer
QA lookup.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.0205 and hit@10 = 0.0400. It ranks a positive first for only 7 queries, and
the median best positive rank is 97. The combination of short queries, long
noisy documents, and graded/multi-positive relevance makes top-10 lexical
ranking difficult.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping T2Ranking pairs, Chinese web
search relevance annotations, multi-positive passage-ranking data, and hard
negatives sampled from passages with the same entity or query phrase but a
lower relevance level. Reranker-style supervision may help because the source
task was designed around passage ranking quality.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic data should start from realistic noisy web passages and create short
Chinese search queries with multiple relevant passages per query. Hard
negatives should preserve query terms while differing in answer specificity,
relevance grade, or intent.
