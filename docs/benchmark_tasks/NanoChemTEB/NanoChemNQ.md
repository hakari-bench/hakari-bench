## Overview

`NanoChemNQ` is the chemistry-filtered Natural Questions retrieval task from
ChemTEB. Queries are real English search-style questions whose relevant answers
come from Wikipedia passages about chemistry, biochemistry, materials, or
chemical-adjacent scientific topics. The retriever must identify the paragraph
that supplies the requested fact, even when nearby paragraphs from the same
article or topic contain stronger lexical overlap.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[ChemTEB: Chemical Text Embedding Benchmark, an Overview of Embedding Models
Performance & Efficiency on a Specific Domain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.00532)
defines ChemNQRetrieval as a retrieval task in ChemTEB and states that the
retrieval category uses a chemistry-related subset of Natural Questions and
HotpotQA with nDCG@10 as the main metric. The BASF dataset card says
ChemNQRetrieval starts from `mteb/nq`, then filters for chemistry-related queries
by starting from the Wikipedia chemistry category and traversing linked articles
up to three levels deep.

[Natural Questions: A Benchmark for Question Answering Research](https://aclanthology.org/Q19-1026/)
introduced questions drawn from real anonymized Google search queries. Annotators
were shown a question with a Wikipedia page from the top five search results and
selected a long answer, usually a paragraph, and a short answer when present.
That provenance explains why the Nano queries read like lowercase search
queries rather than polished benchmark questions.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 27 queries, 10,000 documents, and 35 positive qrel rows.
Queries average 54.00 characters and ask about biochemical suffixes, ATP
hydrolysis, electron transport, periodic-table blocks, iron absorption, hops, and
crystal lattices. Documents average 481.24 characters and are Wikipedia passages
that often include surrounding explanatory context rather than just the answer.

Seven queries have multiple positives, with a maximum of three positives. The
observed positives are frequently paragraph-level evidence, so a passage can be
relevant even if the answer phrase is embedded in broader context about
metabolism, enzymes, periodic-table notation, or chemical energy.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.3616
and hit@10 = 0.4815. BM25 ranks 8 positives first and finds a positive in the
top 10 for 13 of 27 queries. This is notably harder for lexical retrieval than
the ChemHotpotQA Nano split despite the shorter questions.

The hard cases often involve a correct article but wrong paragraph. For the
periodic-table block-name question, BM25 retrieves another "Block (periodic
table)" passage before the paragraph explaining sharp, principal, diffuse, and
fundamental. For the ATP question, BM25 retrieves an ADP paragraph before the
ATP hydrolysis paragraph that states the products. Relation and evidence
specificity matter more than article-level topicality.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Natural Questions long-answer
retrieval pairs, chemistry-filtered Wikipedia QA, biomedical and chemistry fact
retrieval, and hard negatives from the same article or scientific topic. Training
should exclude the ChemNQ evaluation queries, qrels, and positive passages used
by this split.

Because these are real search-style questions, training should include noisy
lowercase queries, incomplete phrasing, and entity-free wording. Paragraph-level
supervision is important: a model should learn to pick the specific paragraph
that answers a relation, not merely the most topical article.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-question generation, use non-evaluation Wikipedia passages about
chemistry, biochemistry, materials, enzymes, periodic trends, and related
science topics. Generate short search-like questions that ask for a specific
fact in the passage without copying the passage wording too closely.

For joint generation, create compact Wikipedia-style paragraphs and natural
search questions over them. Include hard negatives from the same article,
compound class, pathway, or physical concept that omit the requested answer. Do
not use Nano evaluation queries or positive passages as seeds.
