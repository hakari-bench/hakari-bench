## Overview

`NanoR2MEDIIYiClinical` is an English clinical case retrieval task from R2MED.
Queries are translated, de-identified patient records from the IIYi online
consultation platform, and documents are other clinical cases. The task asks a
retriever to find cases with the same or diagnostically useful condition from
long structured clinical presentations.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[R2MED: A Benchmark for Reasoning-Driven Medical Retrieval](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.14558)
describes IIYi-Clinical as part of R2MED's clinical case retrieval task. The
paper reports crawling 10,000 anonymized patient records from more than ten
departments on the IIYi online consultation platform, applying strict
de-identification and privacy-preserving processing, and translating the data
into English with GPT-4o-mini.

R2MED groups IIYi records by diagnostic label using rule-based matching. One
case becomes the query, while remaining cases in the same diagnostic group form
the initial positive set. The paper then applies task filtering, quality
filtering, query formulation, relevance assessment, and expert review, with the
diagnosis acting as the latent bridge between query and relevant cases.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 129 queries, 10,000 documents, and 457 positive qrels.
Queries average 2,584.10 characters, and documents average 5,042.31 characters.
The data is substantially longer than the PMC-Clinical split and usually uses
structured sections such as chief complaint, present illness, past history,
physical examination, auxiliary examination, and treatment.

Each query has 3.54 positives on average, with a median of 3 and a maximum of
6 positives. Sampled cases include pregnancy complications, cerebrovascular
events, gynecologic infections, post-abortion abdominal pain, digestive disease,
and neurological or cardiovascular presentations. The translated clinical style
is sometimes formulaic, and de-identification markers appear in the text.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.1246
and hit@10 = 0.3798. It ranks 18 positives first and finds a positive inside
the top 10 for 49 of 129 queries. Sparse retrieval benefits from repeated
clinical section headings and symptoms, but long cases also contain many
distractor details.

Observed failures include gynecologic, gastrointestinal, and neurological cases
where the top BM25 document shares complaint location or symptoms while the
positive is tied by diagnosis. Some positives rank near the bottom of the
provided candidate list, showing that case similarity based on diagnosis is not
captured reliably by surface overlap.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping diagnosis-labeled clinical case
retrieval, translated or native-English case similarity pairs, de-identified
consultation records, and hard negatives with similar chief complaints but
different diagnoses. Multi-positive objectives are important because most
queries have several relevant cases.

Training should exclude R2MED IIYi-Clinical evaluation queries, qrels, and
positive cases. Because the source is a consultation platform, overlap and
privacy checks should be stricter than for generic encyclopedia data.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-question generation, use non-evaluation de-identified case
records and remove diagnosis or outcome statements from the query while keeping
symptoms, labs, imaging, history, and treatment context. Generate positives as
cases with the same diagnosis but varied presentation.

For joint generation, create clusters of structured clinical records sharing a
diagnosis and add hard negatives with similar demographics or chief complaint
but a different condition. Synthetic data should preserve clinical section
structure and realistic measurements. Do not seed generation with Nano
evaluation cases or positives.
