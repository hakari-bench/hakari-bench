## Overview

`NanoIFIRFire` is a legal precedent retrieval task from IFIR's FIRE-derived
legal subset. Queries are long English case summaries or citation-context
instructions, and documents are prior Indian Supreme Court case documents. The
retriever must find prior cases that support the judicial reasoning or precedent
need.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[IFIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.04644) describes its FIRE2017 legal subset as
retrieving prior cases that support the reasoning process for the current case.
The paper also notes that legal instructions can exceed 1,024 tokens because
they include lengthy legal cases, creating a long-instruction stress test for
retrievers.

[Overview of the FIRE 2017 IRLeD Track](https://ceur-ws.org/Vol-2036/T3-1.pdf)
states that the Precedence Retrieval task asks systems to retrieve relevant or
citable prior case documents for Indian Supreme Court cases. Legal texts are
long and structurally complex, and legal practitioners may care about recall
over many documents.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 167 queries, 1,739 documents, and 563 positive qrels.
Queries average 3,279.47 characters, and documents average 27,167.60
characters. Most queries have multiple positives: 156 of 167 queries have more
than one relevant prior case.

The corpus consists of long Supreme Court judgments with citations, legal
principles, and procedural summaries. Queries are summaries of the current case
and the legal issue being researched.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.3704 and hit@10 = 0.7365. BM25 ranks a positive first for 65 queries. This is
easier than the AILA Nano split for BM25, but still demands long-document legal
matching beyond surface words.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping IRLeD precedent retrieval,
Supreme Court citation-prediction pairs, legal case similarity, and long-context
legal retrieval data. Hard negatives should share broad legal topics or statutes
but not the citable reasoning role.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate current-case summaries with legal issues, cited principles, and
procedural posture. Positive prior cases should be citable for the legal issue.
Include hard negatives with similar legal language but different holdings or
material facts.
