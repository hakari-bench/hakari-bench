## Overview

`NanoIFIRScifact` is an instruction-following scientific literature retrieval
task. Queries are scientific claims, and documents are scientific article titles
and abstracts. The retriever must find abstracts that support, refute, or provide
evidence for the claim according to the instruction.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[IFIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.04644) uses SciFact-open for the scientific
literature domain. It turns claim-evidence retrieval into instruction-following
retrieval by adding requirements such as finding supporting evidence, refuting
evidence, or research-objective-specific passages.

[SciFact: A Dataset and Benchmark for Scientific Claim Verification](https://aclanthology.org/2020.emnlp-main.609/)
introduces scientific claim verification. It asks systems to select abstracts
from the research literature that support or refute a scientific claim and to
identify rationales. The paper reports 1,409 expert-written scientific claims
verified against 5,183 abstracts.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 43 queries, 10,000 documents, and 255 positive qrels. Queries
average 73.60 characters, and documents average 1,452.55 characters. Every
query has multiple positives, with a median of 5 and maximum of 24.

Observed claims cover biomedical mechanisms, cell biology, cancer risk,
receptors, stem cells, and gene expression. Documents are article titles and
abstracts rather than full papers.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.7820 and hit@10 = 1.0000. BM25 ranks a positive first for 35 of 43 queries.
This split is lexically favorable compared with the other IFIR subsets, because
scientific claims often repeat distinctive technical terms used in relevant
abstracts.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping SciFact claim-evidence pairs,
scientific abstract retrieval, citation intent retrieval, and hard negatives from
the same biomedical topic. Training should distinguish evidence retrieval from
truth classification and preserve multiple positives.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate atomic scientific claims and abstracts with explicit mechanisms,
entities, and findings. Include both supporting and contradicting evidence.
Hard negatives should share entities or methods but not satisfy the claim or
instruction polarity.
