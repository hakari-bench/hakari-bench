## Overview

`spanish_passage_s2_p` is the document-level version of the Spanish Passage
Retrieval health dataset. Queries are Spanish health questions, and documents
are full Spanish health-related web pages. The retriever must find documents
that contain relevant answer passages for consumer health needs.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

The [Spanish Passage Retrieval dataset page](https://mklab.iti.gr/results/spanish-passage-retrieval-dataset/)
describes a test collection for Spanish-speaking users with health information
needs about baby care, vaccination, and low back pain. It reports 10,037
health-related Spanish web documents, 37 topics expressed as 167 natural
language questions, and manual passage-level relevance assessments pooled from
multiple systems. The associated ECIR 2019 publication is
`A Test Collection for Passage Retrieval Evaluation of Spanish Health-Related Resources`.

The S2P variant retrieves the source web document that contains relevant
passages, rather than retrieving only the short passage.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 167 Spanish queries, 7,501 documents, and 996 positive
qrels. Queries average 67.56 characters and documents average 2,710.85
characters. Almost every query has multiple positives: 165 of 167 queries have
more than one relevant document.

Sampled questions ask about newborn weight, emergency contacts, first pediatric
checkups, going outside with a baby in hot weather, and vaccine cost. Documents
are consumer-health articles with headings, explanatory paragraphs, and
procedure-like advice.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.4831 and hit@10 = 0.8922. BM25 often finds at least one relevant page, but
some sampled positives are deep in the ranking, including ranks 93 and 100.

The task rewards Spanish medical vocabulary and paraphrase handling. Lexical
overlap helps for terms such as `vacunas`, `bebé`, and `espalda`, but the
consumer question may use wording different from the medical page.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping Spanish consumer-health QA,
Spanish medical FAQ retrieval, and document-level pairs from health web pages.
Training should exclude this test collection's evaluation questions, qrels, and
source pages likely to overlap with Nano.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Spanish health web pages and consumer questions about baby care,
vaccination, emergencies, and low back pain. For S2P, the positive should be
the full page containing the answer passage. Use multiple positives per query
when several pages answer the same information need.
