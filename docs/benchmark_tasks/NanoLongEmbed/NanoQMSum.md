## Overview

`NanoQMSum` is the QMSum meeting-transcript retrieval task in LongEmbed.
Queries are query-focused meeting summaries or information requests, and
documents are long meeting transcripts. The retriever must find the meeting
whose discussion supports the requested summary.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[QMSum: A New Benchmark for Query-based Multi-domain Meeting Summarization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.05938)
introduces query-focused meeting summarization over 232 meetings and 1,808
query-summary pairs. The paper describes three domains: AMI product meetings,
ICSI academic meetings, and parliamentary committee meetings. It also presents
a locate-then-summarize setup, where relevant utterances are first found before
generating the query-focused summary.

[LongEmbed: Extending Embedding Models for Long Context Retrieval](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.12096)
uses QMSum summaries as retrieval queries and meeting transcripts as candidate
documents. Because a summary may describe decisions or discussion outcomes
rather than quote the transcript, the task tests long-context evidence finding
in multi-speaker conversations.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 English queries, 197 meeting transcripts, and 200
positive qrels. Every query has one positive. Queries average 446.33
characters, and documents average 53,335.82 characters. Sampled queries include
remote-control design meetings, Welsh and Canadian parliamentary committee
discussions, and academic speech-recognition meetings.

Transcripts contain speaker labels, disfluency markers, partial words, and long
turn-by-turn discussions. Queries are often abstractive summaries of one topic
inside a larger meeting, so the positive transcript may share only some of the
query's exact wording.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.7132 and hit@10 = 0.8300. BM25 ranks 121 positives first and 166 in the top
10. In the sampled data, one relevant remote-control project meeting is at rank
14, while several others are ranked first or second.

The score reflects a mixed lexical setting. Product-design terms and named
institutions help BM25, but short summaries of decisions, parliamentary
answers, or meeting outcomes can be separated from the exact transcript wording
by disfluencies and speaker interaction.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping QMSum train examples, meeting
summarization corpora with query or topic annotations, and retrieval pairs that
match user information needs to complete meeting transcripts. Training should
exclude QMSum test items, Nano queries, qrels, and positive transcripts likely
to overlap with this evaluation.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-question generation, use non-evaluation meeting transcripts and
generate query-focused summaries over agenda items, decisions, disagreements,
and action items. For joint generation, create multi-speaker transcripts with
disfluencies, roles, topic shifts, and a concise query focused on one topic.
Do not seed generation from Nano evaluation queries or positives.
