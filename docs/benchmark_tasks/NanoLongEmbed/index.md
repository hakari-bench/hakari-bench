## Overview

NanoLongEmbed is the compact long-context retrieval group derived from
LongEmbed. It tests whether a retriever can select the correct long document
when the evidence may be buried in books, scripts, meeting transcripts,
Wikipedia passage bundles, or synthetic long contexts. The group includes both
real long-document retrieval tasks and synthetic passkey or needle settings.

This group is different from ordinary passage retrieval. Documents are often
tens of thousands of characters long, and `NanoNarrativeQA` includes whole books
or movie scripts averaging more than 300,000 characters. A successful model must
retain enough signal from long documents to distinguish the correct source, not
only match a short passage.

## Details

### What the Original Group Measures

[LongEmbed: Extending Embedding Models for Long Context Retrieval](https://aclanthology.org/2024.emnlp-main.47/)
introduces retrieval tasks designed for embedding long documents and long
contexts. NanoLongEmbed keeps six compact splits from that benchmark:
NarrativeQA, SummScreen, QMSum, 2WikiMultiHopQA, Passkey, and Needle.

The tasks divide into two families. NarrativeQA, SummScreen, QMSum, and
2WikiMultiHopQA are real or dataset-derived retrieval settings where a query
must identify the relevant story, episode transcript, meeting transcript, or
Wikipedia evidence bundle. Passkey and Needle are synthetic long-context probes:
the positive document contains a small answer-bearing statement inserted into
otherwise long context.

### Subtask Coverage

- **NanoNarrativeQA:** short story questions retrieve whole books or movie
  scripts.
- **NanoSummScreenFD:** episode recaps retrieve long screenplay-style
  transcripts from the ForeverDreaming subset.
- **NanoQMSum:** query-focused meeting requests retrieve long meeting
  transcripts.
- **Nano2WikiMultihopQA:** multi-hop questions retrieve long concatenated
  Wikipedia evidence documents.
- **NanoPasskey:** synthetic person-to-passkey queries retrieve the long context
  containing the matching passkey statement.
- **NanoNeedle:** synthetic fact queries retrieve the document containing an
  inserted answer-bearing needle.

All six subtasks are English natural-language retrieval tasks. The current Nano
qrels are single-positive for every query.

### Observed Group Profile

The task pages report 998 queries, 998 positive qrels, and 2,788 split-local
candidate documents. Query length averages 242.81 characters when weighted by
query count. SummScreen and QMSum have the longest queries because recaps and
meeting information requests are longer than direct QA questions.

Document length is the defining feature: the split-local document-weighted mean
is 71,544.99 characters. `NanoNarrativeQA` is the most extreme, with documents
averaging 326,753.00 characters. Even the shortest group documents are still
long compared with normal passage retrieval: `NanoPasskey`,
`NanoSummScreenFD`, `NanoNeedle`, and `Nano2WikiMultihopQA` all average tens of
thousands of characters.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate columns, NanoLongEmbed has
query-weighted BM25 nDCG@10 = 0.8099 and hit@10 = 0.9108. The strongest lexical
task is `NanoSummScreenFD` (nDCG@10 = 0.9746, hit@10 = 0.9950), where recaps and
episode transcripts often share distinctive character, event, or scene terms.
`Nano2WikiMultihopQA` is also strong at 0.9515 nDCG@10.

The harder tasks are `NanoNeedle` (0.6852), `NanoNarrativeQA` (0.6910), and
`NanoQMSum` (0.7132). These require finding a small fact inside a long context,
matching a question to a full narrative source, or connecting a meeting request
to the right transcript. BM25 can exploit rare terms, but long documents dilute
signals and can contain many off-topic lexical matches.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes long-document QA pairs, story or script question
answering, meeting transcript retrieval, multi-hop Wikipedia retrieval, and
synthetic long-context passkey or needle data. Training should preserve full
document length or realistic truncation behavior; reducing every document to a
short answer passage would miss the purpose of this group.

Training should exclude NanoLongEmbed evaluation queries, qrels, and positive
documents. Source datasets such as NarrativeQA, SummScreen, QMSum, and
2WikiMultiHopQA should be split-audited before being used for supervised
training.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic data should create long documents first, then insert or identify
grounded facts, events, names, or meeting decisions. Passkey and Needle-style
data should vary the position of the evidence, include distractor facts, and
avoid making the answer trivially unique only by a single rare token. For
realistic long-document training, use stories, transcripts, meeting logs, and
Wikipedia bundles with queries that require selecting the correct whole source.

Do not seed synthetic generation with NanoLongEmbed evaluation documents or
queries. Positives should be long enough that the task remains long-context
retrieval rather than short-passage lookup.
