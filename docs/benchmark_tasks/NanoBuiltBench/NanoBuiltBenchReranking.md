## Overview

`NanoBuiltBenchReranking` is an English built-asset reranking task. Queries are
IFC-style entity names plus definitions, and documents are candidate Uniclass
product descriptions. Unlike the broader retrieval split, this task focuses on
ranking a smaller candidate set containing multiple relevant and hard negative
product descriptions. It measures whether a model can prioritize the most
relevant built asset classes for manual review or downstream alignment.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Benchmarking pre-trained text embedding models in aligning built asset information](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.12056)
introduces six BuiltBench datasets across clustering, retrieval, and reranking.
The [Scientific Reports version](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-09052-5)
explains that reranking addresses the practical need to reduce domain-expert
verification effort by prioritizing the most relevant documents among a set of
potential matches.

The paper uses IFC product titles or descriptions as queries and Uniclass
product descriptions as documents. Ground truth comes from NBS-published
cross-classification mappings between Uniclass records and equivalent IFC
entities. Reranking differs from retrieval by narrowing the scope to smaller
positive and negative subsets chosen for diversity and difficulty; the original
benchmark reports MAP as the primary reranking metric.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 82 queries, 2,898 documents, and 574 positive qrel rows. Each
query has exactly 7 positive documents, so the task has a uniform multi-positive
structure. Queries average 138.28 characters and usually include both an IFC
entity name and a short parent type, for example `Nail (Mechanical Fastener
type)` or `Electric Cooker (Electric Appliance Type)`.

Documents average 309.04 characters and describe Uniclass product classes such
as corrugated fasteners, commercial electric grills, expansion joints, food
mixers, or gas venting grilles. Candidate negatives are often very close in the
classification hierarchy, which makes this a precision-oriented alignment task
rather than a broad corpus search task.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.2389
and hit@10 = 0.7439. It ranks 19 positives first and finds at least one positive
inside the top 10 for 61 of 82 queries. The high hit rate means BM25 often finds
a relevant candidate somewhere near the top, but the lower nDCG reflects weak
ordering among seven positives and many close hard negatives.

Observed hard cases include glue queries where rivets rank above tile adhesives,
flexible pipe segment queries where drainage pipes outrank medical gas copper
tubing, and sink queries where waste components outrank scrub-up troughs. These
are realistic reranking failures: the wrong candidate can share system,
material, or use context while missing the mapped class.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping IFC-to-Uniclass reranking sets,
BIM product-catalog matching, construction taxonomy crosswalks, and hard
negative pairs from neighboring product classes. Because every query has seven
positives, listwise or multi-positive reranking objectives are better aligned
than binary single-positive training.

Training should exclude NanoBuiltBenchReranking evaluation queries, qrels, and
candidate descriptions. If using BuiltBench or MTEB reranking data, remove
overlapping IFC query IDs and Uniclass candidate IDs before evaluating.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-question generation, use non-evaluation product descriptions and
generate concise IFC-style entity labels with parent type context. Generate
multiple relevant descriptions per query when the classification class naturally
maps to several products.

For joint generation, create candidate sets containing several positives plus
hard negatives from nearby construction categories. Hard negatives should share
material, service system, or installation context but differ in function or
class. Do not seed generation with Nano evaluation query or candidate text.
