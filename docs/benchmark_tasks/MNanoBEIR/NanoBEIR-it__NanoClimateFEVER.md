## Overview

CLIMATE-FEVER is a climate-science fact-checking retrieval task.
`NanoBEIR-it__NanoClimateFEVER` is the Italian MNanoBEIR version: Italian
translated climate claims must retrieve Italian translated evidence passages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[CLIMATE-FEVER](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.00614) extends FEVER-style claim
verification to climate change claims and evidence. [BEIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08663)
uses it as a fact-checking retrieval task, and [MMTEB](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.13595)
provides the multilingual embedding benchmark context for this Italian split.

### Observed Data Profile

The sampled Italian Nano task has 50 queries, 3,408 documents, and 148 positive
qrel rows. Most queries have multiple positives: the average is 2.96, with a
range from 1 to 5. Queries are compact climate claims, while documents are
longer encyclopedia-style evidence passages averaging 1,743.14 characters.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.2700 and hit@10 = 0.6200. The median first-positive rank is 4. Lexical cues
help for entities such as glaciers, sea level, or carbon footprint, but evidence
retrieval is not trivial because claims may require paraphrase and scientific
context.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping climate claim-evidence pairs,
scientific fact-checking retrieval data, and Italian or multilingual climate
science QA. Exclude CLIMATE-FEVER, BEIR, NanoBEIR, and translated records likely
to overlap with the evaluation evidence.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Italian climate claims from non-evaluation evidence passages and pair
them with evidence-bearing documents. Hard negatives should share climate terms
but fail to support or refute the exact claim.
