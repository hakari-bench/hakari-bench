## Overview

FEVER is a fact verification evidence retrieval task. `NanoBEIR-it__NanoFEVER`
uses Italian translated claims to retrieve Italian translated Wikipedia
evidence passages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FEVER: a Large-scale Dataset for Fact Extraction and VERification](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05355)
introduced claims paired with Wikipedia evidence for support, refute, or not
enough information labels. BEIR frames FEVER as retrieval of evidence before
verification, and MMTEB provides the multilingual setting for this Italian
version.

### Observed Data Profile

The sampled task has 50 queries, 4,996 documents, and 57 positive qrels. Most
queries have one positive document; 6 queries have multiple positives. Queries
average 50.14 characters and documents average 1,290.36 characters.

### BM25 Difficulty

The dataset BM25 column gives nDCG@10 = 0.7776 and hit@10 = 0.9000. Exact
entity and title overlap often makes the first relevant passage easy, but robust
retrieval still needs to handle translated entity names and claim paraphrases.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping FEVER-style claim-evidence pairs,
Wikipedia evidence retrieval, and Italian or multilingual fact-checking data.
Exclude FEVER, BEIR, NanoBEIR, and overlapping translated Wikipedia evidence.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate short Italian claims from non-evaluation Wikipedia passages and pair
them with evidence paragraphs. Add hard negatives from the same entity page or
nearby entities.
