## Overview

FiQA is a financial question-answer retrieval dataset. `NanoBEIR-it__NanoFiQA2018`
uses Italian translated personal-finance questions to retrieve Italian
translated answer passages from the FiQA retrieval task.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FiQA 2018](https://doi.org/10.1145/3184558.3192301) was created for financial
opinion and question answering data. BEIR uses its retrieval version as a
finance-domain retrieval task, and MMTEB provides the multilingual benchmark
context for the Italian adaptation.

### Observed Data Profile

The sampled Italian task has 50 queries, 4,598 documents, and 123 positive
qrels. Queries average 74.58 characters and often ask practical tax, investing,
loan, and pricing questions. Documents average 1,005.11 characters and are
forum-style financial answers.

### BM25 Difficulty

BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.2633 and hit@10 = 0.4800. The median first-positive
rank is 12, so lexical matching alone is weak. Good models need financial
domain semantics and answer-style matching.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping financial QA, Italian finance forum
retrieval, tax and investing question-answer pairs, and multilingual finance
retrieval data. Exclude FiQA, BEIR, NanoBEIR, and translated answer passages
that may overlap.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate Italian finance questions from non-evaluation answer passages, keeping
the questions realistic and specific. Hard negatives should share financial
terms but answer a different decision or jurisdictional issue.
