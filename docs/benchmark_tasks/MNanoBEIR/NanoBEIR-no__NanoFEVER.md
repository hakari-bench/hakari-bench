## Overview

FEVER is a fact verification evidence retrieval task. `NanoBEIR-no__NanoFEVER`
uses Norwegian translated claims to retrieve Norwegian translated Wikipedia
evidence passages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FEVER: a Large-scale Dataset for Fact Extraction and VERification](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05355)
introduced claims paired with Wikipedia evidence for support, refute, or not
enough information labels. BEIR frames FEVER as evidence retrieval before
verification, and MMTEB provides the multilingual context for this Norwegian
version.

### Observed Data Profile

The sampled task has 50 queries, 4,996 documents, and 57 positive qrels. Most
queries have one positive document; 6 queries have multiple positives. Queries
average 46.02 characters and documents average 1,166.53 characters.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.7396 and hit@10 = 0.9000. Entity and title overlap often helps, but translated
claims still require matching paraphrased evidence passages.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping FEVER-style claim-evidence pairs,
Wikipedia evidence retrieval, and Norwegian or multilingual fact-checking data.
Training should exclude FEVER, BEIR, NanoBEIR, and overlapping translated
Wikipedia evidence.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate short Norwegian claims from non-evaluation Wikipedia passages and pair
them with evidence paragraphs. Add hard negatives from the same entity page or
nearby entities.
