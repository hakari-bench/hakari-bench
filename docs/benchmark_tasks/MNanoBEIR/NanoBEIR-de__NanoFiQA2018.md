## Overview

FiQA-2018 is a financial opinion mining and question answering benchmark.
`NanoBEIR-de__NanoFiQA2018` is the German MNanoBEIR version: each query is a
German translated personal-finance question, and the system must retrieve a
German translated answer post or answer-like passage. The task tests practical
financial advice retrieval under specialized terminology and user-question
phrasing.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[WWW'18 Open Challenge: Financial Opinion Mining and Question
Answering](https://doi.org/10.1145/3184558.3192301) describes FiQA as a Web
Conference 2018 challenge over financial data. Task 2, the source for this
retrieval task, is opinion-based question answering over financial data. The
paper reports that the Opinion QA collection was built from StackExchange posts
under the Investment topic and evaluated with ranking metrics such as nDCG and
MRR.

[BEIR: A Heterogeneous Benchmark for Zero-shot Evaluation of Information
Retrieval Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08663) includes FiQA-2018 as a
financial question answering retrieval task. [MMTEB: Massive Multilingual Text
Embedding Benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.13595) provides the
multilingual benchmark setting for this German compact version.

### Observed Data Profile

The sampled German Nano task has 50 queries, 4,598 documents, and 123 positive
qrel rows. It is multi-positive: queries average 2.46 positives, 28 of 50
queries have more than one positive, and one query has 15 positives. The
average query length is 74.62 characters, and the average document length is
1,052.17 characters.

The inspected queries ask practical financial questions about government debt,
investing through another person, calendar spreads in non-margin accounts,
whether most investors lose money, and whether to pay credit-card debt or take
an employer retirement-plan match. Positive documents are translated
community-answer style passages with assumptions, caveats, examples, and
financial-product details.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.1740 and hit@10 = 0.3600. Only 11 of 50 queries have a positive ranked first,
and the median first-positive rank is 40. Every query has at least one positive
within the top 100, but the top 10 often misses the relevant answer.

This is a hard sparse-retrieval task because German finance questions often ask
for a decision or mechanism while answer posts explain the reasoning in
different words. Strong retrievers should identify the financial action,
instrument, account type, and advice relation, rather than simply matching
terms like debt, investing, option, or employer contribution.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping FiQA question-answer pairs,
German or multilingual financial community QA, personal-finance forum retrieval
data, brokerage and tax FAQ retrieval pairs, and domain QA about debt,
retirement accounts, investing, banking, and options. Training should exclude
FiQA, BEIR, NanoBEIR, or translated records likely to overlap with these
evaluation questions or answers.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

For document-to-query generation, start from non-evaluation financial advice
answers and generate German user questions that the answer resolves. Include
account types, jurisdictions, investment products, debt, taxes, and numeric
assumptions. Synthetic positives should answer the decision or explanation
need, not merely mention the same product.
