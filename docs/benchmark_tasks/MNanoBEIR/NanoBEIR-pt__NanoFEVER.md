## Overview

FEVER is a Wikipedia evidence retrieval task for factual claims.
`NanoBEIR-pt__NanoFEVER` uses Portuguese translated claims and Wikipedia-style
evidence passages.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[FEVER](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05355) was built for fact extraction and
verification over Wikipedia, where systems must retrieve evidence for claims
before deciding whether they are supported or refuted. BEIR evaluates the
retrieval part, and MMTEB gives the multilingual benchmark context.

### Observed Data Profile

The sampled task has 50 queries, 4,996 documents, and 57 positive qrels. Most
queries have one positive, with a small multi-positive tail. Queries average
49.04 characters, and documents average 1,245.71 characters.

### BM25 Difficulty

BM25 reaches nDCG@10 = 0.8043 and hit@10 = 0.9400. The median first-positive
rank is 1.0, indicating that Portuguese translated FEVER claims often preserve
strong named entities and lexical evidence cues.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful data includes non-overlapping claim-evidence retrieval, Portuguese
Wikipedia evidence mining, and multilingual fact-checking. Exclude FEVER, BEIR,
NanoBEIR, and direct translations of evaluation claims or evidence pages.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate short Portuguese factual claims from non-evaluation encyclopedia
passages. Pair each claim with the passage that verifies or refutes it, and use
hard negatives from related entities or neighboring events.
