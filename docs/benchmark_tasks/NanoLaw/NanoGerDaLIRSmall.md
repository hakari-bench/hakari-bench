## Overview

`NanoGerDaLIRSmall` is a German legal information retrieval task. Queries are
German legal passages, and documents are German court decisions; the retriever
must identify the cited or otherwise relevant legal document.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[GerDaLIR: A German Dataset for Legal Information Retrieval](https://aclanthology.org/2021.nllp-1.13/)
presents a German legal IR dataset built from Open Legal Data. The paper states
that GerDaLIR contains 123K queries, at least one relevant document per query,
and a collection of 131K case documents. It constructs query-document relevance
from passages that cite known collection documents and emphasizes that passage
queries better reflect realistic legal research than whole-document queries.

The [MTEB GerDaLIRSmall card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mteb/GerDaLIRSmall)
notes that the small version keeps only documents with corresponding queries,
creating a smaller corpus for evaluation.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 9,969 documents, and 235 positive qrels. Query
passages average 889.88 characters, while documents average 19,706.82
characters. Most queries have one positive document, but 29 queries have
multiple positives; the maximum is 4.

Observed text contains anonymized dates and references such as `[DATE]` and
`[REF]`, German legal reasoning, court-tenor sections, and long administrative,
tax, labor, asylum, and civil-law decisions.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.5848 and hit@10 = 0.7200. It ranks a positive first for 101 queries. Lexical
matching is often strong because cited passages and target documents share
legal terminology, but long documents and anonymized references still leave many
queries outside the top 10.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes German legal citation retrieval, passage-to-case
retrieval, in-domain German legal language modeling, and hard negatives from
cases sharing statutes or court domains. Models should handle long German
documents and passage-level cues.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Synthetic queries should look like German legal reasoning passages with
citations, anonymized dates, and doctrinal statements. Positives should be full
judgments that support or are cited by the passage. Hard negatives should share
legal provisions and court vocabulary but concern a different dispute.
