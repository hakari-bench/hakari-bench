## Overview

`NanoLegalSummarization` is an English contract-summary retrieval task. Queries
are plain-English summaries of terms-of-service or contract clauses, and
documents are the corresponding legal text snippets.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[Plain English Summarization of Contracts](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.00424)
introduces a dataset of legal text snippets paired with plain-English
summaries. The paper states that the dataset was built from online resources
such as TL;DRLegal and TOS;DR, manually checked for summary quality, and
designed around heavy abstraction, compression, and simplification. It reports
446 sets of contract sections and summaries, and shows that extractive
summarization baselines struggle because reference summaries often use language
that does not appear in the original contract text.

The [MTEB legal_summarization card](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mteb/legal_summarization)
frames the resource as 439 pairs of contracts and their summaries from those
sources. In NanoLaw, the retrieval direction is summary-to-contract-snippet.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 438 documents, and 345 positive qrels. Queries
average 103.06 characters and are simplified, lowercased statements about user
rights, restrictions, tracking, real-name policies, age limits, or prohibited
conduct. Documents average 606.16 characters and are contract clauses or
shorter legal-language snippets.

The split is moderately multi-positive: 56 queries have multiple positives, the
average is 1.73 positives per query, and the maximum is 11.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.5400 and hit@10 = 0.7350. It ranks a positive first for 90 queries. BM25 does
well when summaries reuse words like `spam`, `real name`, or `age`, but the
paper's abstraction finding is visible in examples where the plain-English
summary paraphrases the contract clause.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes non-overlapping contract-summary pairs,
terms-of-service simplification, clause-to-description retrieval, and hard
negatives from clauses covering adjacent user rights.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate plain-English summaries of real-looking contract clauses. Positives
should be the legal clause that entails the summary; hard negatives should share
keywords but differ in permission, obligation, scope, or exception.
