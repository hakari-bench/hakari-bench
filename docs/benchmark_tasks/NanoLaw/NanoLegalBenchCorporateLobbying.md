## Overview

`NanoLegalBenchCorporateLobbying` is an English retrieval task derived from
LegalBench corporate lobbying. Queries are congressional bill titles or bill
descriptions, and documents are bill titles plus summaries that should match
the query.

## Details

### What the Original Data Measures

[LegalBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.11462) describes `corporate_lobbying`
as an issue-spotting task: deciding whether a proposed congressional bill may be
relevant to a company based on information about the bill and the company's SEC
10-K self-description. The paper says the task involves identifying the legal
consequences of a bill and whether those consequences matter to the company's
business model, structure, or activities.

The [LegalBench corporate_lobbying page](https://hazyresearch.stanford.edu/legalbench/tasks/corporate_lobbying.html)
lists 500 manually labeled samples, with legal reasoning type `Issue-spotting`.
The MTEB retrieval version used here converts the bill side into a retrieval
problem over bill titles and summaries.

### Observed Data Profile

The Nano split has 200 queries, 319 documents, and 200 positive qrels. Each
query has exactly one positive document. Queries average 179.67 characters;
documents average 1,157.21 characters and usually contain a bill title followed
by a compact legislative summary.

Observed examples cover health privacy, U.S.-China security, appropriations,
retirement plans, and offshore energy. The text is legal/policy language but
much shorter than the case-law tasks.

### BM25 Difficulty

Using the dataset-provided BM25 candidate column, BM25 reaches nDCG@10 =
0.8757 and hit@10 = 0.9700. It ranks a positive first for 154 queries. This is
the easiest NanoLaw split for BM25 because query and document often share bill
phrasing and distinctive policy terms.

### Training Data That May Help

Useful training data includes bill-title to bill-summary retrieval, legislative
search, policy-domain hard negatives, and corporate lobbying or regulatory
issue-spotting data. For dense models, negatives should include bills from the
same committee or topic area.

### Synthetic Data Guidance

Generate bill titles or short legislative objectives as queries. Positive
documents should include the formal bill title and a summary of provisions.
Hard negatives should mention the same agency, statute, or industry but change
the bill's actual policy effect.
